# 11. Common Misconceptions and Mistakes (Local)

The persistent local myths in the North Alabama VA market and the factual corrections, with a focus on misconceptions that show up specifically in this geography.

## "VA loans take forever to close in Alabama"

A VA purchase loan in North Alabama closes routinely in 21 to 35 days from a complete contract when run by a VA-experienced local lender. The 60-day-plus VA closes that built this reputation came from out-of-state lenders unfamiliar with attorney-state closings, NPMA-33 cycles, and AHFA reservation timing. Locally experienced lenders close VA on conventional-comparable timelines.

## "VA offers don't get accepted in Madison"

Madison County is one of the highest-VA-share buyer pools in the southeastern US because of Redstone Arsenal. Sellers and listing agents in this market routinely accept VA offers. The categorical "I won't take a VA offer" stance filters out a meaningful slice of qualified buyers. Where VA offers struggle, the issue is usually price, terms, or pre-approval letter quality, not the VA structure itself. The dual-bucket framing of allowable closing costs (uncapped) plus seller concessions (capped at 4% of reasonable value) gives sellers more room to make the deal work than the conventional 3% concession cap allows.

## "VA appraisals always come in low"

VA appraisers use the same comparable-sales methodology as conventional appraisers. The VA does not instruct appraisers to value low. Low appraisals happen across loan types in the same market conditions; they are not a VA-specific phenomenon. In Madison County's stable-to-mildly-appreciating early-2026 market, appraisal-gap issues are infrequent for properties priced in line with comparable sales. Where appraisals come in low, the underlying issue is the contract price relative to the market, not the VA program.

## "VA-required repairs will bankrupt the seller"

The VA Minimum Property Requirements are specific and finite, focused on safety, sanitation, and structural soundness. The most commonly triggered MPR repairs in North Alabama are: peeling exterior paint on pre-1978 homes (lead-based paint MPR), missing handrails on stairs of three or more risers, exposed electrical, active termite infestation requiring treatment, and obvious roofing or structural issues. Most of these would be flagged on a conventional or FHA appraisal as well, and most would be flagged by any reasonable home inspection. The "VA-required repairs" reputation is largely a misattribution to VA of issues that any reasonable buyer would want addressed.

## "I have to use a Huntsville-based lender for a VA loan in Alabama"

Not technically. Any VA-approved lender licensed in Alabama can originate a VA loan on an Alabama property. The local-experience advantage is real, but the requirement is just a state-licensed lender. Alabama-resident borrowers can use Alabama-licensed lenders headquartered out of state, and out-of-state borrowers PCSing into Alabama can use either Alabama-licensed or their current state's lender during the orders-to-arrival window. The practical risk of an out-of-state lender unfamiliar with this market lies in NPMA-33 dynamics, attorney-state closings, AHFA reservation timing, and BAH-grossing-up math.

## "AHFA Salute Our Soldiers is the Alabama veteran program"

This is the most important single correction in this packet. **Alabama does not have a program called "Salute Our Soldiers."** That program belongs to the Florida Housing Finance Corporation. Alabama's actual VA-paired down payment assistance is the **AHFA Step Up program**, which is a 10-year repayable second mortgage (not a forgivable grant) of up to $10,000 or 4% of the purchase price. Content that uses "Salute Our Soldiers" as the Alabama program name is referencing the wrong state. See Part 2 Section 7 for the corrected program details.

## "Active-duty buyers can't close before they arrive at Redstone"

The VA owner-occupancy rule allows a spouse to fulfill the occupancy requirement when the active-duty service member cannot personally occupy because of military duty. The buyer's spouse can take possession at closing while the service member is still en route or completing duties at the prior station. Both spouses sign loan documents (or a properly executed power of attorney can be used), and the spouse moves in. The 60-day reasonable-time-to-occupy guideline applies, with documented intent to occupy. This is one of the most common Redstone-area scenarios.

## "VA buyers can't pay any closing costs"

VA buyers can pay most allowable closing costs. The VA's non-allowable fees list is finite (most lender administrative junk fees, attorney's fees for the lender's side, brokerage commissions, certain inspection fees on new construction). Allowable buyer-paid costs include the funding fee, origination fee within the 1% cap, appraisal, title insurance, recording, prepaid escrow, discount points, and termite inspection in states (including Alabama) where VA Circular 26-22-11 allows the buyer to pay. The myth that "VA buyers can pay nothing" is wrong; the actual rule is that some specific items are restricted, and most others can be allocated either to buyer or seller through standard contract negotiation.

## "If I have a pending disability claim, I have to wait until it's finalized before closing"

Not necessarily. A proposed or memorandum rating issued by VA before the loan closing date qualifies the borrower for the funding fee exemption. Active-duty service members with evidence of a Purple Heart award before closing also qualify regardless of disability rating status. For pending claims that are likely to be approved with an effective date before closing, a buyer who pays the funding fee at closing may be eligible for a refund after the rating is finalized retroactively. Route specific exemption-eligibility questions to the VA Regional Loan Center serving Alabama (St. Petersburg) or to a county Veterans Service Officer.

## "USDA is always cheaper than VA for rural Madison County"

USDA Rural Development is a separate program with separate rules. For VA-eligible borrowers buying a USDA-eligible address in Hazel Green, Harvest, Meridianville, Toney, New Market, Owens Cross Roads, or other unincorporated North Alabama, both programs are usable and the comparison is borrower-specific. VA wins for most borrowers on flexibility (no income cap for most files, no annual fee equivalent to USDA's $0.35 annual guarantee fee on the unpaid principal balance). USDA can win for low-income borrowers below the USDA income limit, or where the buyer is not VA-eligible at all and a non-veteran spouse is the primary qualifying borrower. A local lender experienced in both programs should run the comparison rather than defaulting to either.

## "I can only use my VA benefit once"

VA entitlement is restorable and can be used multiple times across a service member's life. Common scenarios in the Redstone-area buyer pool: (1) full restoration after paying off a prior VA loan and selling the property, (2) one-time restoration where the prior VA loan was assumed by a non-VA buyer who substituted entitlement, (3) bonus-tier (second-tier) entitlement use to retain a prior-station home as a rental while buying at Redstone with remaining entitlement, (4) repeat use after restoration following a divorce-related quitclaim. The "use once and done" version of the rule is wrong. Mechanics of entitlement restoration live in the federal-mechanics file.

# 12. Do/Don't Guidance (Local)

Action-oriented guidance specific to North Alabama VA buyers, sellers, and Realtors. Federal-rule mechanics (escape clause, entitlement math, funding fee exemption documentation) live in the mechanics file.

## DO

**Start the lender conversation at receipt of orders, not at arrival in Huntsville.** PCS timelines compress quickly. The buyer who starts pre-approval at orders-receipt has 30 to 90 days of runway; the buyer who waits until arrival has the relocation-window pressure stacked on top of every other moving-target item.

**Pull your Certificate of Eligibility (COE) early and verify exemption status before closing.** The COE is the document the lender uses to determine funding fee tier and exemption status. If a service-connected disability rating exists but the COE has not been refreshed, the lender will collect the funding fee at closing and the buyer has to recover it through a post-closing refund process. Pulling and verifying the COE early avoids that.

**Use a VA-experienced lender familiar with Alabama attorney-state closings, NPMA-33 dynamics, AHFA reservation timing, and BAH grossing-up math.** Local-experience matters more than national brand. The bar to clear is competence in this market, not advertising volume.

**Pair AHFA Step Up with VA when income, credit, and purchase price fit.** The pairing reduces cash-to-close meaningfully and adds an MCC option for ongoing federal income tax credit. The trade-off is the 10-year second mortgage repayment obligation; for buyers with the income to support both payments, the math is favorable.

**Request the NPMA-33 inspection early enough in the contract period that any treatment-and-reinspection cycle can complete before the scheduled closing.** A 30-day contract that orders the inspection on day 25 will struggle if active infestation is found.

**File the Alabama homestead exemption by December 31 of the tax year of purchase.** The basic exemption itself is modest in dollar terms, but the reclassification to Class III (10% assessment rate vs 20% Class II) is meaningful, and the deadline is non-negotiable. File at the county Tax Assessor or Revenue Commissioner. Madison County Probate phone for related questions: 256-532-3330.

**Verify USDA eligibility on rural addresses through the official USDA eligibility map.** For unincorporated Madison County addresses, USDA-eligibility is property-specific (census-tract designation). The official map at https://eligibility.sc.egov.usda.gov/eligibility/welcomeAction.do is the authoritative source.

**Document active-duty owner-occupancy intent in writing.** When the spouse is fulfilling owner-occupancy on the service member's behalf, the lender file should include orders-to-Redstone documentation, the service member's intent-to-occupy statement, and any POA instruments executed at the closing attorney's direction.

**Verify a lender's NMLS registration and Alabama state license before sharing personal information.** Use NMLS Consumer Access at https://www.nmlsconsumeraccess.org. Alabama-specific lender licensure is also visible on the Alabama State Banking Department's website.

## DON'T

**Don't accept unsolicited IRRRL offers without verification.** The VA does not solicit refinances by phone, mail, or email. Any unsolicited contact claiming VA affiliation is fraudulent or misleading. Common predatory patterns: skip-a-payment offers, inflated escrow refund promises, fake government branding, cash-out churning. Verify any solicitation by independently calling the VA Home Loan Specialist at 1-877-827-3702 or checking the lender's NMLS record.

**Don't let a national online lender unfamiliar with NPMA-33 and attorney-state closings run an Alabama VA file without local oversight.** The lender does not have to be Alabama-headquartered, but the file needs someone who understands the Alabama transaction, whether that is the loan officer, the closing attorney, or a local Realtor coordinating both sides.

**Don't assume VA appraisals always come in low.** The narrative is dated and not supported by current data. Treat appraisal risk as a market-condition question, not a VA-program question.

**Don't pay for "paperwork assistance" or upfront fees from any lender or third party.** Legitimate lenders issue Loan Estimates and other federally-required disclosures before collecting fees, and they do not charge for "VA paperwork assistance" outside the standard origination process.

**Don't misrepresent occupancy intent to qualify for VA financing on a property that will function as a rental or second home.** VA is a primary-residence program. Occupancy fraud puts the buyer's VA benefit at risk and can carry criminal exposure.

**Don't take VA disability-rating claim advice from a Realtor or lender.** Route disability-rating questions to VA, a county Veterans Service Officer (Madison County VSO is at https://www.madisoncountyal.gov/departments/veterans-services), or a VA-accredited representative.

**Don't take SCRA interpretation advice from a Realtor or lender as the final word.** Route SCRA questions to JAG, military legal assistance, or the CFPB. The 6% interest-rate cap on pre-service debt and the foreclosure-protection rules are fact-specific and not appropriate for non-attorney interpretation.

**Don't recommend a specific lender, builder, or attorney by name in marketing copy without RESPA-compliant disclosure.** Respect the kickback and referral-fee restrictions in 12 USC 2607.

**Don't quote a specific homeowner's insurance premium for a buyer's specific property in marketing copy.** Insurance premiums vary by carrier, roof age, claims history, deductible, and Fortified status. Cite ranges from third-party aggregators (e.g., $1,500 to $2,963 per year for $300,000 dwelling coverage in Huntsville per current published averages) and direct buyers to get carrier-specific quotes.

**Don't promise that a VA buyer will qualify, will close on a specific date, or will be approved at a specific rate** without those terms being locked in writing. This applies as much to social media posts as it does to formal advertising.

**Don't reproduce VA seals, logos, or DoD branding** in any marketing material or content without VA authorization. The fake-government-correspondence pattern is one of the most heavily enforced areas of VA-mortgage advertising violations.

**Don't allow PCS-urgency framing to push a buyer into a rushed decision** without pre-approval, BAH math, school-district verification, and a baseline neighborhood walk-through.

# 13. Compliance and Phrasing Caveats (Local)

What to say carefully and what not to say at all when producing VA-content for North Alabama. This section is operational guidance, not legal advice; route formal compliance questions to a mortgage compliance attorney or Princeton Mortgage's compliance team.

## Military-Discount Language

**Avoid:** "Special veteran rate," "exclusive military pricing," "veterans-only discount," "military-discount mortgage."

**Why:** VA loan rates are set by the lender based on the same market-rate factors that apply to conventional loans. There is no government-mandated "veteran rate." Marketing language that implies a special, exclusive, or below-market rate available only to veterans creates the false impression that the VA establishes loan pricing or that the lender has access to a special veterans-only product. This is exactly the type of misrepresentation that has triggered CFPB enforcement in the 2020 sweep.

**Alternative phrasing:** "VA-eligible borrowers may qualify for $0-down financing with no monthly mortgage insurance," "rates available to VA-eligible borrowers based on current market conditions and individual credit profile," "the VA loan program features that distinguish it from conventional financing include..."

## "VA-Approved" Misuse

**Avoid:** "VA-approved Realtor," "VA-approved home," "VA-approved community," "VA-approved neighborhood."

**Why:** The VA approves lenders, condominiums (project-level), and certain other specifically-defined entities. The VA does not approve Realtors, individual homes (the appraisal verifies MPR compliance, not a property-level approval), or neighborhoods. "VA-approved Realtor" is a particularly common misuse; there is no such credential issued by the VA.

**Acceptable phrasing:** "VA-experienced Realtor," "Realtor who works regularly with VA buyers," "VA-eligible property" (when discussing a specific property that has been verified to meet MPR), "VA-approved condominium project" (when referring to a specific project on the VA's approved-projects list).

## PCS-Urgency Manipulation

**Avoid:** "Don't lose your house to a slow lender," "you only have 30 days to find a home before your report date," urgency framing tied to military timelines.

**Why:** PCS timelines are real, but the urgency framing crosses into manipulation when it is used to push a buyer into a faster decision than the buyer can responsibly make. CFPB enforcement actions on military-targeted advertising have specifically called out urgency tactics that pressure quick decisions without adequate disclosure.

**Acceptable phrasing:** "PCS timelines compress the homebuying window; starting pre-approval at receipt of orders gives you the most flexibility," "if you're PCSing in, here's what to expect on the lender side."

## The Never-Guarantee List

Never say (in copy, social media, or conversation):

- "Your loan is guaranteed."
- "You will close on [date]."
- "Your rate will be [X]%."
- "The funding fee will be [exempt / waived]" (without verified COE).
- "VA will [approve / deny] your loan."
- "Your appraisal will come in at [X]."
- "Sellers in Madison County [always / never] accept VA offers."

These statements are either factually impossible, dependent on borrower-specific verification, or misrepresent the relationship between the lender and the VA. They also expose the lender, Realtor, or content-publisher to UDAAP liability under the Consumer Financial Protection Act.

## Hedge Language That Is Required

When discussing rates, fees, eligibility, exemptions, or program features, hedge with:

- "...subject to VA approval and lender underwriting."
- "...based on individual credit profile and current market conditions."
- "...as of [date]; verify current with [official source]."
- "...not all borrowers will qualify."
- "...consult [lender / VA / VSO / JAG / closing attorney] for advice specific to your situation."

These are not just compliance theater. They preserve accuracy and protect the buyer from acting on an oversimplification.

## NMLS and Alabama State License Disclosure

Mortgage loan originators serving Alabama borrowers must be NMLS-registered and Alabama-licensed (or registered as a federal-charter exempt employee through the SAFE Act). Marketing copy that names a specific loan originator should include the NMLS number per the SAFE Act. The Alabama State Banking Department, Bureau of Loans (https://banking.alabama.gov/bureau-of-loans/) is the state regulator.

NMLS Consumer Access for borrower verification: https://www.nmlsconsumeraccess.org.

## VA Advertising Guidance (VA Form 26-0585)

The VA's Advertising Guidelines for Fair Housing (VA Form 26-0585) outlines the fair-housing requirements for VA-approved-lender advertising (https://www.vba.va.gov/pubs/forms/vba-26-0585-are.pdf, verified 05-09-2026).

The VA's proposed advertising and solicitation rule (RIN 2900-AJ86, originally published December 8, 2000) prohibits private lenders from falsely stating or implying that an advertisement was issued by, or at the direction of, the VA or any other US Government entity (https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2000/12/08/00-31291/loan-guaranty-advertising-and-solicitation-requirements, verified 05-09-2026).

Advertising patterns the VA and CFPB have specifically flagged as deceptive:

- Solicitations stating they are from the "Government Loans Program" or referencing a VA case number to imply VA origination.
- Solicitations stating that the lender is "authorized" by VA or "VA-affiliated."
- Solicitations using VA seals, logos, or government-correspondence visual cues.
- Solicitations claiming "Penalty for private use, $300" envelope language.
- Solicitations describing an introductory adjustable rate as "fixed."
- Solicitations advertising skipped payments as a benefit of an IRRRL.

## AHFA Disclaimer Expectations

AHFA-related marketing copy should accurately represent program features. Specifically:

- The Step Up second mortgage is a **repayable** 10-year term, not a forgivable grant.
- The income limit ($159,200 as of current 2026 guidelines), credit floor (640), and DTI cap (45%) are program-defined and should not be hedged or misrepresented.
- "AHFA Salute Our Soldiers" is incorrect. The correct program name is **AHFA Step Up** (or First Step, MCC, or Affordable Income Subsidy Grant for the layered structures).
- "Down payment assistance" should be hedged as "down payment and/or closing cost assistance" because Step Up funds are flexible across both buckets.

Verify all AHFA program features at https://www.ahfa.com/programs/homeownership before publication. Program parameters change.

## CFPB Military-Marketing Enforcement Patterns

The CFPB's 2020 sweep against deceptive VA-mortgage advertising produced consent orders against nine companies and over $4.4 million in civil money penalties (source: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/newsroom/consumer-financial-protection-bureau-settles-ninth-mortgage-company-address-deceptive-loan-advertisements-sent-servicemembers-and-veterans/, verified 05-09-2026).

Specific cases for reference:

- **Low VA Rates LLC**: $1,800,000 civil penalty. Direct-mail advertisements misrepresented APR, failed to disclose required credit terms under Regulation Z, made misleading debt-elimination claims, and made misleading hypothetical-loan-term comparisons.
- **ClearPath Lending Inc.**: $625,000 civil penalty. Misrepresented credit terms, used the consumer's current lender's name in a misleading way, failed to adequately disclose ClearPath's name and non-affiliation with the consumer's existing lender.
- **Sovereign Lending Group**: $460,000 civil penalty. Misrepresented credit terms, described an introductory adjustable rate as "fixed," created false government-affiliation impression, used the consumer's current lender's name misleadingly.
- **Prime Choice Funding**: $645,000 civil penalty. Sent millions of misleading mailers; same pattern as Sovereign plus false "property assessment" claims and misleading hypothetical-versus-actual loan term comparisons.
- **Accelerate Mortgage LLC**: Consent order for false, misleading, and inaccurate advertisements lacking required disclosures, in violation of CFPA, MAP Rule, and Regulation Z.
- **Go Direct Lenders Inc.**: Consent order. Advertised lower rates than the company was prepared to offer; described ARMs as "fixed"; falsely implied no appraisal, asset, or income documentation needed; falsely claimed records of consumer property-value increases; created false government-affiliation impression.
- **PHLoans.com Inc.**: $260,000 civil penalty. Misrepresented existence and amount of fees and costs; failed to disclose required credit terms.
- **Village Capital & Investments LLC**: $268,869 civil penalty. Cost-comparison worksheets that inflated future principal owed under existing mortgage, underestimated future payments on the proposed refinance, and overestimated total monthly benefit (source: https://natlawreview.com/article/cfpb-settles-va-lender-regarding-claims-deception, verified 05-09-2026).
- **RMK Financial**: Used VA logos, seal, and "Penalty for private use, $300" envelope language to imply VA origination; misrepresented fixed-versus-variable rate; hid disclosures in fine print on the back of mailers (source: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/blog/the-va-doesnt-send-you-mortgage-ads/, verified 05-09-2026).

Common violations across the sweep: CFPA prohibition against deceptive acts and practices, the Mortgage Acts and Practices Advertising Rule (MAP Rule, 12 CFR Part 1014), and Truth in Lending Act Regulation Z (12 CFR Part 1026).

The pattern that emerges from these cases: **a piece of VA-mortgage marketing copy that produces a false impression of government affiliation, misrepresents credit terms, fails to disclose required fee or rate information, or describes an adjustable-rate product as fixed will trigger CFPB enforcement.** A clean compliance posture requires accurate credit-term disclosure, clear lender identification, no government-imitation visual elements, and accurate fixed-versus-adjustable characterization.

# 15. Output Requirements Wrap-Up

## 15a. FAQ in Answer-First AEO Format (FAQPage Schema-Ready)

The FAQ below is organized by topic for easier downstream FAQPage schema deployment. Each Q is in first-person buyer voice; each A leads with the answer in 1 to 2 sentences, then provides supporting detail.

### Eligibility and PCS

**Q: Can I use my VA loan if I'm PCSing to Redstone Arsenal?**

A: Yes. PCSing to Redstone is one of the most common scenarios the VA loan program was designed to serve. Active-duty buyers commonly use VA at Redstone whether they have full entitlement or partial entitlement. Start the lender conversation at receipt of orders.

**Q: Can my spouse close on the house before I arrive at Redstone?**

A: Yes. The VA owner-occupancy rule is satisfied when the spouse occupies the home on the active-duty service member's behalf during the period the service member cannot personally occupy because of military duty. Both spouses sign loan documents (or a properly executed power of attorney can be used for the deployed or absent service member).

**Q: Can a Reservist drilling at the Decatur armory get a VA loan?**

A: Yes, if the Reservist meets the federal-rule qualifying-service thresholds. Documentation differences from active-duty borrowers typically include a Statement of Service or NGB-22. Work with a lender experienced in Reserve VA files.

**Q: I'm retiring at Redstone. Should I use VA now or wait until I'm out?**

A: Depends on your disability rating status, retirement timeline, and post-retirement income picture. Two specific considerations: the VA funding fee is exempt for veterans receiving compensation for a service-connected disability; the VA retirement-occupancy exception allows up to 12 months delay in personal occupancy. Route disability questions to VA, not to the lender or Realtor.

**Q: Can I keep my house at my prior duty station and buy a new one with VA at Redstone?**

A: Yes, in many cases. This is the second-tier (bonus) entitlement scenario. With Madison County's standard $832,750 conforming loan limit and a typical Redstone-area purchase below that level, the math frequently allows zero-down at Redstone even when prior entitlement is partially used. Run the specific calculation with a VA-experienced lender.

### Alabama-Specific Transaction

**Q: Do I need a termite letter for a VA loan in Alabama?**

A: Yes. Virtually every VA transaction in Alabama includes a Wood Destroying Insect Inspection Report (NPMA-33). The seller commonly pays for it. If active infestation is found, treatment by a state-licensed pest control company and a clearance letter are required before closing.

**Q: How long does a VA loan take to close in Alabama?**

A: 21 to 35 days from a complete contract with a VA-experienced local lender. The "VA loans take forever" reputation is dated; experienced Alabama VA lenders close on conventional-comparable timelines.

**Q: Why do I need an attorney at closing in Alabama?**

A: Alabama is an attorney-state for residential closings. Alabama Supreme Court precedent holds that giving legal advice at a real estate closing constitutes the unauthorized practice of law (Coffee County Abstract & Title Co. v. State, 445 So. 2d 852). Closings happen at the closing attorney's office; the attorney handles title examination, deed preparation, and recording.

**Q: How does the Alabama homestead exemption work for active-duty?**

A: The exemption applies to property owners who own and occupy a single-family residence as their primary residence on October 1 of the tax year. Active-duty service members qualify on the same terms as civilian owners. The deadline to apply is December 31 of the tax year. SCRA-related considerations for service members domiciled in another state are fact-specific; route those questions to JAG.

**Q: How much is homeowner's insurance in Huntsville for a $400K home?**

A: Common range for $300K to $400K dwelling coverage runs $1,500 to $3,000 per year, with current published averages around $2,300 to $2,900. North Alabama is a tornado and severe-storm market, not a hurricane market, so premiums are below Gulf Coast Alabama levels. Get quotes from at least three carriers; do not assume one quote is representative.

**Q: Does Strengthen Alabama Homes work for VA buyers?**

A: Yes. The program provides grants up to $10,000 for FORTIFIED roof retrofits on owner-occupied single-family homes. The grant pays the contractor directly upon FORTIFIED designation. Wind-portion premium discounts of approximately 25% to 55% are commonly available on FORTIFIED-designated homes.

### AHFA and Down Payment Assistance

**Q: What's AHFA Salute Our Soldiers and can I use it with a VA loan?**

A: "Salute Our Soldiers" is a Florida program, not an Alabama program. Alabama's equivalent VA-paired down payment assistance is the AHFA Step Up program, which provides up to $10,000 or 4% of the purchase price as a 10-year repayable second mortgage. Step Up now offers a VA-paired first mortgage option in addition to FHA and conventional pairings.

**Q: How do I qualify for AHFA Step Up with a VA loan?**

A: Income limit is $159,200 regardless of household size or location; minimum credit score 640; debt-to-income maximum 45%; homebuyer education course required at least once before closing for at least one borrower. Apply through an AHFA-participating lender, not directly with AHFA.

**Q: Is the AHFA Step Up assistance forgivable?**

A: No. Step Up is a 10-year repayable second mortgage. The borrower makes monthly payments on the second mortgage during the 10-year term. There is no balloon at the end. Specific recapture conditions can apply when the property is sold within nine years and the borrower's income has increased substantially.

### Funding Fee and Disability

**Q: Do disabled veterans pay the VA funding fee in Alabama?**

A: No. The VA funding fee is exempt for veterans receiving VA compensation for a service-connected disability, regardless of the rating percentage. Surviving spouses receiving DIC, active-duty Purple Heart recipients, and service members with proposed or memorandum ratings before loan closing are also exempt.

**Q: What is the VA funding fee for 2026?**

A: For first-use purchase with no down payment, 2.15%. For subsequent-use purchase with no down payment, 3.30%. For an IRRRL, 0.50%. For a cash-out refinance, the fee mirrors the purchase-loan first-or-subsequent tiers. The funding fee is now tax-deductible as of 2026 for those who itemize.

**Q: What if my disability rating comes through after closing?**

A: If the rating's effective date is before the loan closing date, you may be eligible for a funding fee refund. Contact the VA Regional Loan Center serving Alabama (St. Petersburg, 1-877-827-3702) and submit VA Form 26-8937 (Verification of VA Benefits) to establish retroactive exemption.

### Local Market

**Q: Will sellers accept my VA offer in Madison County?**

A: Yes. Madison County is one of the highest-VA-share buyer pools in the southeast because of Redstone Arsenal. Sellers and listing agents in this market routinely accept VA offers. The dual-bucket concession structure (uncapped allowable closing costs plus 4% concession cap) gives sellers more room than a conventional 3% concession cap.

**Q: What's the BAH for Redstone Arsenal in 2026?**

A: Redstone falls in the Huntsville MHA AL003. 2026 BAH ticked down approximately 0.9% from 2025. Typical 2026 amounts: E-6 with dependents around $2,079/month; O-3 with dependents around $2,226/month. Look up exact current rates at the official DoD BAH Calculator.

**Q: Where do most active-duty Army families live around Redstone?**

A: Three main clusters: Madison City (top-rated public schools, 12 to 18 minute commute to Gate 9); the Hampton Cove and Owens Cross Roads side (Huntsville City Schools magnet options); and north Madison County (Harvest, Hazel Green, Meridianville, Toney) for larger lots and lower price points within Madison County Schools.

**Q: What are the 2026 VA loan limits for Madison County?**

A: $832,750 standard conforming loan limit for Madison County and all other North Alabama counties. No North Alabama county is currently designated high-cost. Full-entitlement borrowers can exceed the limit without a down payment if the lender approves the larger loan amount; partial-entitlement borrowers are constrained by remaining-entitlement math.

### Compliance and Trust

**Q: How do I verify a lender is legitimate before sharing personal information?**

A: Check NMLS Consumer Access at https://www.nmlsconsumeraccess.org. Every legitimate mortgage loan originator in the US is NMLS-registered. Confirm active licensing in Alabama. The Alabama State Banking Department maintains a separate state registry.

**Q: What do I do if I get an unsolicited VA refinance offer in the mail?**

A: Treat it as a red flag. The VA does not solicit refinances by phone, mail, or email. Common predatory patterns include skip-a-payment offers, inflated escrow refund promises, and fake government branding. Verify any solicitation by independently calling the VA Home Loan Specialist at 1-877-827-3702 or checking the lender's NMLS record. Report deceptive offers at https://www.consumerfinance.gov/complaint/ or the VA OIG hotline at 1-800-488-8244.

### Never-State Statements

These statements should not appear in any content from this packet:

- "Your VA loan is approved" (without verified COE and lender approval).
- "You will close on [specific date]."
- "VA approves [Realtors / specific homes / specific neighborhoods]."
- "AHFA Salute Our Soldiers" (wrong state).
- "VA disability rating advice" (route to VSO/VA, not lender/Realtor).
- "SCRA interpretation as lender or Realtor" (route to JAG).
- "You're exempt from the funding fee" (without verified COE).
- "This solicitation is from the VA" (the VA does not solicit refinances).
- Any quoted homeowner's insurance premium for a specific buyer's specific property.
- Any statement implying the lender is government-affiliated, government-endorsed, or operating under VA authority beyond standard VA-approved-lender status.

### Risk Areas Requiring Extra Care

- **Disability-rating claims and exemption documentation.** This is a borrower-VA relationship, not a borrower-lender relationship. Do not produce content that implies the lender controls the exemption determination.
- **SCRA interpretation.** This is a fact-specific federal-statute interpretation; route to JAG.
- **Predatory IRRRL solicitation framing.** Educate borrowers on the patterns; do not produce content that itself uses the patterns. Avoid skip-a-payment marketing, government-imitation visuals, fake urgency.
- **Property-specific advice.** Generic guidance is appropriate for broad audiences; specific advice on a specific property requires the lender's underwriting context and the closing attorney's title context.
- **Active-duty borrower under deployment, mobilization, or TDY.** Specific occupancy, SCRA, and orders-related questions require lender, JAG, and possibly the gaining unit's coordination.
