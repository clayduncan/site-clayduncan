# 6. Alabama-Specific Transaction Realities for VA Buyers

How Alabama closing customs and state law reshape VA transactions. The federal rule on MPRs, allowable fees, and the escape clause is in the mechanics file. This section covers what changes when those rules meet Alabama-specific practices.

## The NPMA-33 Wood Infestation Inspection Report

The NPMA-33 (officially titled the Wood Destroying Insect Inspection Report) is required on virtually every VA transaction in Alabama. The form is national, copyrighted by the National Pest Management Association, and approved for both FHA and VA loans (sources: https://www.npmapestworld.org/your-team-tools/npma-forms-npma3399a99b/npma-forms-information/, https://www.hud.gov/sites/dfiles/OCHCO/documents/NPMA33.pdf, both verified 05-09-2026). The Alabama prevalence is the local part. Subterranean termite pressure across North Alabama is high enough that VA appraisers, lenders, and buyers all expect the report.

**Who orders it.** Typically the buyer or the buyer's lender orders the inspection through a state-licensed pest control company, though sellers sometimes order it proactively to strengthen a listing.

**Who pays.** Negotiable. VA Circular 26-22-11 (effective for transactions on or after the circular date) allows the Veteran borrower to pay the termite inspection fee in any state, including Alabama, where the inspection is required (source: valoannetwork.com/va-termite-pest-inspections/, accessed 05-09-2026). In practice in this market, the seller commonly pays for the WDI inspection because the customary-fee allocation in Alabama purchase contracts often defaults that way and because seller-paid pest inspection is routine. Verify against the federal rule in the mechanics file before publishing specific guidance.

**What happens when WDI is found.** If the inspector finds active infestation or visible damage, treatment and corrective action are recommended on the report. The lender will typically require:

- Treatment by a state-licensed pest control company.
- Documentation of treatment (receipt, treatment record, sometimes a renewable bond).
- A reinspection or "clearance" letter confirming the issue has been addressed.

Treatment cost for subterranean termites on an average North Alabama home generally runs $500 to $1,500 (source: valoannetwork.com/va-termite-pest-inspections/, accessed 05-09-2026; verify with local pest companies for current pricing). The seller commonly pays this in VA transactions because the borrower is restricted from paying certain costs.

**Timing in the closing process.** The NPMA-33 inspection should be completed early enough in the contract period that a treatment-and-reinspection cycle can finish before the scheduled closing. Many lenders treat the report as valid roughly 90 days for underwriting (source: valoannetwork.com/va-termite-pest-inspections/, accessed 05-09-2026). If the contract is long-tail (60+ days from inspection to closing), the lender may require a recertification.

**Common findings in older Huntsville and Decatur housing stock.** Older inventory in Five Points, downtown Huntsville, central Decatur, and similar pre-1980 neighborhoods more frequently turns up evidence of prior subterranean termite activity. Active versus old activity matters; an inspector noting "evidence of prior treatment, no active infestation" is a different conversation from "active infestation observed."

## Attorney-State Closings

Alabama is an attorney-state for residential closings. Alabama Code Title 34 prohibits non-attorneys from preparing legal documents for compensation in real estate transactions, and the Alabama Supreme Court has historically held that giving legal advice at a real estate closing constitutes the unauthorized practice of law (sources: cga.ct.gov/2009/rpt/2009-R-0448.htm summary citing Coffee County Abstract & Title Co. v. State, 445 So. 2d 852 (Ala. 1983); freedom-res.com/post/alabama-real-estate-closing-process/, accessed 05-09-2026; cloudwillis.com/2018/09/05/your-guide-to-closing-on-real-estate-in-alabama/, accessed 05-09-2026).

**Where closings happen.** Typically at the closing attorney's office, sometimes at a title company affiliated with an attorney's firm. Some attorneys will travel to a buyer's office or home for refinances and special circumstances. Mobile closings are routine in the Birmingham and Huntsville markets.

**Who chooses the closing attorney.** The buyer typically selects and pays for the closing attorney, though this is negotiable in the contract (source: freedom-res.com/post/alabama-real-estate-closing-process/, accessed 05-09-2026). Lender approval of the closing attorney is often required when the lender has a closing-attorney panel.

**What the closing attorney does in a VA transaction.** Title examination, title insurance issuance, deed preparation, recording coordination through the appropriate county Probate Office, and execution of the VA-specific closing certifications. The closing attorney is a process facilitator, not the buyer's or seller's advocate; in Alabama, the closing attorney does not typically represent either party at the closing table.

**Implications for VA borrowers.** The closing attorney's familiarity with VA-specific certifications, allowable-fee rules, and the seller-concession-versus-closing-cost distinction matters more in this state than in non-attorney states. A closing attorney who runs many VA files knows what the lender will ask for. A closing attorney who runs few VA files may produce avoidable last-minute friction.

## Property Tax Mechanics

Alabama has the lowest effective property tax in the country. The state's effective property tax rate is approximately 0.38% on the median home value (source: propertytaxrates.org/exemptions/alabama/, accessed 05-09-2026). This affects DTI and PITI math favorably for VA buyers. A $400,000 Madison County home generates an annual property tax bill that is meaningfully lower than the same house in Texas, North Carolina, Tennessee, or Georgia.

**The Alabama homestead exemption.** A homestead exemption is available to property owners who own and occupy a single-family residence as their primary residence on October 1 of the tax year. Alabama Code 40-9-19 through 40-9-21 governs the exemption. The deadline to apply is December 31 of the tax year (sources: https://www.revenue.alabama.gov/property-tax/homestead-exemptions/, https://www.madisoncountyal.gov/departments/tax-assessor/homestead-exemptions, both verified 05-09-2026).

The basic (H-1) exemption allows up to $4,000 in assessed value off state property tax and up to $2,000 off county property tax for owners under 65 who are not disabled. The exemption also reclassifies the property at the 10% assessment rate (Class III, owner-occupied) versus the 20% rate (Class II, non-owner-occupied), which is the larger of the two savings in many cases. The basic-exemption dollar savings is approximately $48 per year, but the reclassification savings can be much larger because it changes the assessed value baseline.

**Senior, disabled, and disabled-veteran exemptions.** H-2, H-3, and H-4 exemptions provide additional relief for owners 65+, owners with permanent and total disability, and certain combinations. H-3 (permanent and total disability) exempts the property from all ad valorem taxes when the owner provides VA award letter, Social Security determination, or two physician affidavits documenting the disability. **Do not advise on disability-rating eligibility from the Realtor or lender side.** Route those questions to VA, the county Revenue Commissioner, or the county Veterans Service Officer.

**Filing the exemption county by county.** Each county's Tax Assessor or Revenue Commissioner handles homestead exemption applications. The process typically requires:

- A copy of the recorded deed showing the buyer's name and the correct legal description.
- A driver's license with an address matching the property address (some counties allow alternative documentation if the address is updated within a window after purchase).
- For senior/disability-based exemptions, supporting documentation of age, income, or disability.

Counties for North Alabama VA buyers, with verified pages where available:

- **Madison County Tax Assessor**, 1918 Memorial Parkway NW, Huntsville. Source: https://www.madisoncountyal.gov/departments/tax-assessor/homestead-exemptions, verified 05-09-2026.
- **Limestone County Revenue Commissioner**, https://www.limestonerevenue.net/, verified 05-09-2026.
- **Morgan County Revenue Commissioner**, https://www.morgancountyrevenue.com/, verified 05-09-2026.
- **Marshall County Revenue Commissioner**, verify current URL at the county site before publishing.
- **Jackson County Revenue Commissioner**, verify current URL.
- **DeKalb County Revenue Commissioner**, verify current URL.
- **Cullman County Revenue Commissioner**, https://cullmanrevenuecommissioner.com/, verified 05-09-2026.

**Active-duty exemption considerations under SCRA.** The Servicemembers Civil Relief Act provides certain property-tax protections for active-duty service members domiciled in another state who are stationed in Alabama. Application of those protections to Alabama property tax is fact-specific. **Route SCRA property-tax questions to JAG, military legal assistance, and the county Revenue Commissioner**, never to the lender or Realtor.

## Insurance Realities for VA Buyers in North Alabama

North Alabama insurance is shaped by **tornado and severe-storm exposure**, not by hurricane and wind-pool rules that dominate Gulf Coast Alabama. This produces a distinct insurance profile:

- **Average annual premium.** Estimates from third-party aggregators range from approximately $1,500 to $2,963 per year for $300,000 of dwelling coverage in Huntsville, with the most-cited current average around $2,300 to $2,912 (sources: insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/alabama/huntsville/, bankrate.com/insurance/homeowners-insurance/huntsville-al/, insure.com/home-insurance/how-much-is-homeowners-insurance-in-huntsville-al/, all accessed 05-09-2026). Premiums vary widely by carrier, roof age, claims history, deductible, and Fortified status. Do not publish a specific premium quote.
- **Wind/hail deductibles.** Many Alabama HO-3 policies carry a wind/hail deductible separate from the all-other-perils deductible. In coastal counties this often takes the form of a percentage deductible (1% to 5% of dwelling coverage); in North Alabama the wind/hail deductible is more often a flat dollar amount or a smaller percentage. Verify specifically with the carrier and binder language.
- **The Strengthen Alabama Homes program** (Alabama Department of Insurance). Provides grants up to $10,000 for FORTIFIED roof retrofits on owner-occupied single-family homes. The grant pays the contractor directly upon FORTIFIED designation. The grant program is funded by the Department of Insurance and disbursed quarterly on a first-come-first-served basis (source: https://www.strengthenalabamahomes.com/, verified 05-09-2026).
- **FORTIFIED Roof discount.** Alabama-admitted insurance carriers must offer wind-portion premium discounts on FORTIFIED-designated homes. Discount levels vary by carrier; commonly cited ranges run from approximately 25% to 55% off the wind portion of premium for FORTIFIED Roof or higher designations (source: smarthomeamerica.org/resources/strengthen-alabama-homes, accessed 05-09-2026; aldoi.gov/currentnewsitem.aspx?ID=1321 Hurricane Sally study, accessed 05-09-2026; verify exact discount with the specific carrier).
- **Why North Alabama premiums differ from Gulf Coast Alabama.** North Alabama is not in the Alabama Insurance Underwriting Association (Beach Pool) or other coastal wind-pool program. Premiums are driven by tornado and severe convective storm risk, not hurricane storm-surge or named-storm wind-pool exposure. The result is generally lower base premiums than coastal Mobile and Baldwin counties, but with FORTIFIED roof discounts that have grown in availability and adoption.
- **Insurance binder timing relative to VA closing.** The closing attorney requires a paid-receipt-or-binder showing first-year premium paid (or financed through escrow) at or before closing. The VA does not require flood insurance unless the property is in a Special Flood Hazard Area; if it is, flood coverage is mandatory.
- **Coverage levels VA requires.** VA requires hazard insurance sufficient to cover the lesser of replacement cost or the loan amount. The lender sets the minimum coverage; VA does not specify a fixed dollar amount.
- **Active-duty and sometimes-deployed insured.** Insurers occasionally raise occupancy questions when the insured is on extended TDY or deployment. The active-duty buyer should disclose deployment status to the carrier; insurers generally accommodate active-duty deployment and TDY without policy disruption when properly disclosed.

## Well, Septic, and Rural Property Realities

Many North Alabama properties are on private wells and septic systems. Common locations include unincorporated Madison County (Hazel Green, Harvest, New Market, Meridianville, Toney, Owens Cross Roads), much of Limestone County outside Athens proper, rural Morgan County, Marshall County, Jackson County, and DeKalb County.

**VA MPR water testing.** Federal-rule mechanics, full description, and current testing standards live in the mechanics file. The local relevance: water tests are routine for well-served properties in North Alabama. The buyer should plan for a water test on any private-well property, and the test must come from a sample collected by an appropriate party (often the local health authority or a state-certified lab). Standard VA-required testing screens for total coliforms, lead, nitrates, and nitrites at minimum; additional contaminants may be added by local health authority requirement (sources: valoans.com/articles/va-property-standards-well-water/, veteransunited.com/valoans/well-test/, eppwellsolutions.com/blog/fha-and-va-loan-requirements-for-well-water-a-2025-guide, all accessed 05-09-2026; authoritative reference: VA Pamphlet 26-7).

**Septic inspection requirements.** Properties on septic systems often require a septic inspection or pumping receipt for VA financing. Field practice in North Alabama is for the lender to require either documentation of recent septic service or a current septic-system functioning evaluation. Verify with the lender for the specific transaction.

**Shared wells.** Properties served by a shared well (two or more dwellings on the same well) can meet VA MPRs if the water is safe, the supply is adequate, and the shared-use arrangement is documented. The shared-well agreement is typically a recorded easement or contract.

**How rural-property condition issues commonly surface in VA appraisals.** In Limestone, Morgan, Marshall, Jackson, and DeKalb counties, recurring issues include peeling exterior paint on pre-1978 homes (lead-based paint MPR), missing handrails, exposed electrical, and roofing conditions. None of these is uniquely a VA problem; all of them surface more often in older rural inventory than in newer Madison City suburban inventory.

## Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA) and Military Lending Act (MLA)

**SCRA protections relevant to VA homebuyers.** The SCRA is federal law (50 U.S.C. § 3901 et seq.) and provides several protections that intersect with VA home loans:

- **Interest rate cap on pre-service debt.** Mortgage interest on pre-service debt may be capped at 6% during active-duty service when the service member properly invokes the protection. Mortgages originated during active duty (for example, a VA loan taken out by an active-duty buyer at Redstone) do not typically receive the 6% cap because the debt was incurred during, not before, active duty.
- **Lease termination.** SCRA allows service members to terminate residential leases on the receipt of qualifying orders.
- **Foreclosure protection.** Foreclosure on mortgages incurred before active-duty service is restricted during and for 12 months after active-duty service in many circumstances.

(Sources: https://www.militaryonesource.mil/financial-legal/legal/servicemembers-civil-relief-act/, https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/educator-tools/servicemembers/servicemembers-civil-relief-act/, both verified 05-09-2026.)

**MLA protections relevant to VA homebuyers.** The Military Lending Act (32 CFR Part 232) caps the Military Annual Percentage Rate at 36% on most consumer credit extended to active-duty service members and their dependents. **Mortgage loans secured by an interest in the dwelling are excluded from MLA coverage in most cases**, so MLA does not cap a VA loan's APR by itself. MLA still affects ancillary credit (auto loans, credit cards, payday-style products) used by active-duty buyers. Source: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/educator-tools/servicemembers/military-lending-act/, verified 05-09-2026.

**How these statutes affect lender practice for active-duty borrowers.** A VA-experienced lender screens for SCRA-flagged debts when underwriting and applies the 6% cap to qualifying pre-service debt. An out-of-state lender unfamiliar with the active-duty concentration in this market may miss SCRA-relevant debts. Route SCRA enforcement and interpretation questions to JAG, DoD, or CFPB, never the lender or Realtor as the final word.

# 7. AHFA and VA Pairing

**Naming clarification at the top.** The Alabama Housing Finance Authority (AHFA) does not currently administer a program named "Salute Our Soldiers." That program name belongs to the Florida Housing Finance Corporation. Searches and content built using "Salute Our Soldiers" as the Alabama program name are referencing the wrong state. The correct AHFA programs that pair with VA financing in Alabama are **Step Up**, **First Step**, the **Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC)**, and the **Affordable Income Subsidy Grant** (sources: https://www.ahfa.com/programs/homeownership, https://www.ahfa.com/news/new-year-new-changes-to-step-up-program, both verified 05-09-2026).

If a North Alabama VA buyer asks "What's AHFA Salute Our Soldiers?" the correct response is to clarify the Florida-versus-Alabama naming and walk through the actual AHFA programs.

## Program Status Verification

As of 05-09-2026:

- **AHFA Step Up.** Active. Recently expanded to offer a VA-paired first mortgage option in addition to the longstanding HFA Advantage conventional and FHA pairings (source: https://www.ahfa.com/news/new-year-new-changes-to-step-up-program, verified 05-09-2026).
- **AHFA First Step (Mortgage Revenue Bond program).** Active. Pairs with FHA, VA, USDA, or HFA Advantage conventional first mortgages.
- **Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC).** Active. Federal income tax credit; pairs with AHFA loans or with any 30-year fixed-rate mortgage from a participating lender.
- **Affordable Income Subsidy Grant.** Available with HFA Advantage conventional product.

For VA buyers, the most relevant pairing is **Step Up + VA first mortgage + MCC**, where the program guidelines allow it and where the buyer's income and purchase price fit the program limits.

## Borrower Eligibility (AHFA Step Up, current 2026 guidelines)

- **Income limit.** Applicants earning $159,200 or less are eligible regardless of household size or location (source: bankrate.com/mortgages/alabama-first-time-homebuyer-assistance-programs/, accessed 05-09-2026; verify current limits at ahfa.com).
- **Credit score floor.** Minimum 640 across loan types.
- **Debt-to-income ratio.** Maximum 45%.
- **First-time buyer requirement.** Step Up is available to both first-time and repeat homebuyers. (First Step has more specific first-time-buyer requirements that depend on whether the property is in a target or non-target census tract.)
- **Veterans.** Veterans may have additional flexibility on first-time-buyer status under First Step in non-target areas.
- **Homeownership education course.** Required at least once before closing for at least one borrower on the loan.
- **U.S. citizenship or legal residency** required.

(Source: https://www.dsldmortgage.com/blog/first-step-program-down-payment-assistance-in-alabama/, accessed 05-09-2026, confirmed against ahfa.com program pages.)

## Property Eligibility

- **Primary residence only.** Investment properties and second homes do not qualify.
- **Property types.** Single-family houses and townhouses. Condominiums must be FHA-approved or conventional-approved depending on the first mortgage type.
- **Manufactured housing.** Out of scope for this packet (excluded by prompt).
- **Purchase price limits.** Vary by program and county. Verify current Madison and surrounding county limits at ahfa.com.
- **Owner-occupancy.** Required within 60 days of closing.

## Down Payment Assistance Mechanics

The AHFA Step Up assistance is structured as a **10-year second mortgage**, not a grant, paired with a 30-year fixed-rate first mortgage. The current assistance amount is up to **$10,000 or 4% of the purchase price, whichever is less** (sources: https://www.ahfa.com/news/new-year-new-changes-to-step-up-program; bankrate.com/mortgages/alabama-first-time-homebuyer-assistance-programs/, both accessed 05-09-2026).

- **Form of assistance.** Repayable second mortgage at a fixed rate, 10-year term.
- **Repayment.** Borrower makes monthly payments on the second mortgage during the 10-year term. There is no balloon at the end of the term.
- **Recapture.** Specific recapture conditions can apply when the property is sold within nine years, the borrower's income has increased substantially, and the borrower realizes a gain on the sale. AHFA sends recapture documentation after closing. Most participants do not encounter recapture (source: dsldmortgage.com/blog/first-step-program-down-payment-assistance-in-alabama/, accessed 05-09-2026).
- **Lien position.** Second.

The Step Up second mortgage is **not forgivable**. This distinguishes it from Florida's Salute Our Soldiers PLUS forgivable assistance. Content that describes Alabama's program as "forgivable" is incorrect.

## Pairing Step Up with VA

- **Step Up + VA first mortgage.** Allowed under the 2026 AHFA guidelines (source: https://www.ahfa.com/news/new-year-new-changes-to-step-up-program, verified 05-09-2026).
- **How the funding fee interacts.** The VA funding fee may be financed into the VA first mortgage in the standard way; the Step Up assistance is applied to down payment or closing costs. The funding fee does not consume the Step Up assistance.
- **Combination with seller concessions.** VA's 4% seller concession cap and the unlimited allowable-closing-cost credits both still apply when Step Up is in the structure. Step Up assistance applied to closing costs reduces the cash-to-close burden; seller concessions can layer on top within the federal-rule limits.
- **What this looks like in practice.** A typical Step Up + VA structure for a Madison County buyer might use VA $0-down on the first mortgage, Step Up assistance of up to $10,000 toward closing costs and prepaids, seller concessions toward funding fee or buydown within the 4% cap, and an MCC providing ongoing federal income tax credit. The combined effect is meaningfully lower cash-to-close than VA alone for a buyer who fits the AHFA income and credit guidelines.
- **Pairing with FHA or conventional alternatives.** Step Up also pairs with FHA, USDA, and HFA Advantage conventional. VA is the strongest pairing for most North Alabama veteran buyers because of $0 down and no monthly mortgage insurance, but where VA is not the right product for a specific buyer (for example, a non-veteran spouse on a single-borrower file), the AHFA program still works with the alternative first mortgage.

## Application Process

- **AHFA-participating lender requirement.** Step Up loans are originated through AHFA-approved participating lenders, not directly through AHFA. The list of participating lenders is at ahfa.com.
- **Reservation timing.** The AHFA-participating lender reserves the funds with AHFA when the borrower's file is ready. Reservation is typically made before underwriting submission to lock the assistance allocation.
- **Homebuyer education requirement.** At least one borrower must complete an approved homeownership education course before closing. AHFA approves multiple courses including online options.
- **Closing timeline implications.** AHFA loans add a small administrative layer (reservation, AHFA-specific underwriting confirmations, AHFA closing documents). With a participating lender experienced in AHFA closings, the timeline impact is minimal. With a lender unfamiliar with AHFA, the timeline can extend by a week or more.

# 8. Local Lender Practice and Realtor/Seller Education

How VA transactions actually run in this market.

## Local Lender Practice

**Why working with a local Alabama-licensed lender experienced in VA matters.** A local VA-experienced lender:

- Knows the appraiser panel that VA cases are assigned to in this market and can anticipate how specific appraisers handle MPR ambiguities.
- Knows the AHFA Step Up pairing and the participating-lender reservation process.
- Knows the NPMA-33 dynamics and the local pest control companies whose reports are reliably accepted.
- Knows the local insurance carriers and the binder-timing expectations of the local closing attorneys.
- Knows BAH math for the Redstone-area buyer and applies BAH grossing-up appropriately.
- Knows the Alabama attorney-state closing process and works with closing-attorney panels efficiently.

A national online lender or a lender experienced primarily in non-attorney states is not categorically wrong, but the local-knowledge gap creates avoidable friction. Multiple agents and lenders interviewed for general industry coverage cite out-of-state lender unfamiliarity with NPMA-33, attorney-state closings, and AHFA reservation as the most common cause of delayed Alabama VA closings.

**Predatory IRRRL solicitation patterns that target Redstone-area homeowners.** Veterans in this market are routinely targeted for VA Interest Rate Reduction Refinancing Loan (IRRRL) solicitations, both in the months immediately after closing and on an ongoing basis. The solicitation patterns CFPB and VA have flagged are:

- **Skip-a-payment offers.** "Skip your next two mortgage payments." VA prohibits a lender from advertising the skipping of payments as a means of obtaining cash in an IRRRL. Lenders that advertise this as a benefit are violating VA advertising rules (source: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/blog/cfpb-and-va-warno-va-refinancing-offers-sound-too-good-be-true/, verified 05-09-2026; news.va.gov/43234/va-and-the-consumer-financial-protection-bureau-warn-against-home-loan-refinancing-offers-that-sound-too-good-to-be-true/, accessed 05-09-2026).
- **Inflated escrow refund promises.** "You'll get $3,000 back at closing." The actual escrow refund depends on what is in the account at closing and is often much less than the marketing number.
- **Cost-comparison worksheet manipulation.** Inflating the future principal owed under the existing mortgage; underestimating the future monthly payment on the proposed refinance; overestimating the long-run benefit. CFPB took enforcement action against Village Capital & Investments LLC for exactly these practices (source: natlawreview.com/article/cfpb-settles-va-lender-regarding-claims-deception, accessed 05-09-2026).
- **Fake-VA branding.** Mailers designed to look like government correspondence. The VA does not solicit refinances by phone, mail, or email. Any unsolicited contact claiming VA affiliation is fraudulent or misleading (source: valoannetwork.com/va-irrrl-scam-warning/, accessed 05-09-2026).
- **Aggressive sales tactics.** Daily robocalls, mailers timed to look like checks or bills, pressure to refinance weeks after closing.
- **Cash-out refinance churning.** Repeated cash-out refinances that extract equity through high closing costs, eroding the borrower's position over time.

**The CFPB and VA "WARNO" guidance** (originally issued November 2017, reissued by VA News in November 2017) is the foundational warning document. CFPB has continued enforcement actions against VA refinance lenders for misleading practices through 2024 (source: moaa.org/content/publications-and-media/news-articles/2024-news-articles/finance/8-tips-to-avoid-home-loan-scams-targeting-servicemembers-and-veterans/, accessed 05-09-2026).

**For local content.** Frame IRRRL solicitations as a known risk pattern, not a market-specific anomaly. Direct affected veterans to:

- VA Home Loan Specialist: 1-877-827-3702
- CFPB complaint portal: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/complaint/
- VA Office of Inspector General hotline: 1-800-488-8244 or va.gov/oig/hotline

## Realtor Education

**The myth that VA offers don't get accepted in Madison/Huntsville.** This is a recurring, market-specific misconception. The empirical reality is:

- The North Alabama buyer pool has a high VA share due to the Redstone Arsenal economic ecosystem.
- Listing agents and sellers who categorically reject VA offers are filtering out a meaningful slice of qualified buyers.
- VA offers are competitive on price, can close fast (21-day closes are achievable with experienced lenders), and the seller's net is generally not impaired by the VA structure when concessions are reasonable.

Specific market data verifying VA-offer acceptance rates in Madison County is not readily published as a standalone metric. The Huntsville Area Association of Realtors (HAAR) market reports do not segment by loan type. Qualitative agent-side reporting consistently indicates that VA offers are routinely accepted in this market, especially in price segments where VA buyers are concentrated ($300K to $500K).

**Why some listing agents counsel sellers against VA offers and how to counter that.** The historical Realtor narrative against VA offers cites:

- "VA appraisals come in low." Reality: VA appraisals are not categorically lower than conventional appraisals. The VA appraiser uses the same comparable-sales approach as a conventional appraiser. Where appraisals come in low, the issue is usually the local market, not the VA.
- "VA-required repairs are excessive." Reality: VA Minimum Property Requirements are concrete and finite. They focus on safety, sanitation, and structural soundness, not cosmetic issues. Active termite infestation, peeling exterior paint on pre-1978 homes, missing handrails, and obvious structural issues are the most common MPR-triggered repair requirements. None of these is unique to VA; they typically appear on FHA appraisals as well and would be flagged by any reasonable home inspection.
- "VA buyers can't pay closing costs." Reality: VA buyers can pay most allowable closing costs, can negotiate seller concessions up to 4% of the home's reasonable value, and can layer unlimited allowable-cost credits on top of the 4% concession bucket (source: https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/funding-fee-and-closing-costs/, verified 05-09-2026).

**The Realtor's role in framing the VA escape clause and MPR-repair conversation with sellers.** The federal-rule mechanics of the VA escape clause (38 CFR 36.4304(g)) are in the mechanics file. Local Realtor responsibility:

- Explain to the seller that the VA escape clause protects the buyer if the property fails to appraise at the contract price; it does not function as a no-fault contract terminator.
- Frame MPR repairs as a finite list of items, not an open-ended seller obligation.
- Negotiate repair allocation in good faith, recognizing that VA-allowable-fee restrictions limit what the buyer can pay for.

**North Alabama Realtors with significant VA-buyer experience.** The HAAR membership directory at https://huntsvillealabamarealtors.com/ (verified 05-09-2026) is the starting point for identifying member agents. There is no public certification specifically called "VA-experienced Realtor"; the closest comparable credential is the Military Relocation Professional (MRP) certification through the National Association of Realtors. MRP credential is helpful but does not by itself indicate VA-loan-transaction experience. Agent-side experience is best verified by asking specific questions about recent VA transactions.

## Seller Education

**Common seller objections to VA offers and the factual responses.**

- **"Perceived appraisal risk."** Response: VA appraisers use the same comparable-sales methodology as conventional appraisers; appraisal risk is a market reality, not a VA-specific reality.
- **"Repair concerns."** Response: MPRs are concrete and finite; most MPR repair requirements would also be triggered by a conventional or FHA inspection.
- **"Closing timeline."** Response: VA closings in this market routinely run 21 to 30 days with experienced lenders; conventional closings are not categorically faster.

**The 4% concession + uncapped customary closing costs framing for sellers.** This is the most actionable seller-education talking point. Sellers do not have to think of "VA buyer" as a higher-cost buyer. They can think of it as: "I can pay all the buyer's allowable closing costs (no VA cap on that bucket) AND I can offer up to 4% of the home's reasonable value in concessions (separate cap). The total looks larger than a conventional 3% concession cap, but in practice the funding-fee math, the seller-paid termite, and other line items all fit comfortably in the dual-bucket structure" (source: https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/funding-fee-and-closing-costs/, verified 05-09-2026; jd.mortgage/va-seller-concessions/, accessed 05-09-2026).

**Repair flexibility under MPRs vs the urban legend of "VA-required repairs."** The VA does not maintain a "list of required repairs." MPRs are stated as outcome standards (safe, sound, sanitary). The appraiser identifies items that fail to meet those standards. A reasonable seller and a reasonable buyer working with an experienced VA-fluent Realtor can usually negotiate a repair list that closes the deal without either party feeling bled.

## VA-Specific Market Framing

- **Most of the North Alabama VA market** sits comfortably below the $832,750 conforming loan limit, so partial-entitlement math typically does not bind on full-entitlement borrowers.
- **For partial-entitlement borrowers** (most commonly the active-duty PCSer who is keeping a prior-station home as a rental), the math depends on the entitlement already used at the prior station. In a $329K Madison County purchase scenario with a partial-entitlement buyer carrying a $415K VA loan at the prior station, the buyer can frequently still execute zero-down at Redstone, but should verify with the lender at the orders-receipt stage, not after offer.
- **New construction inventory in Madison and Limestone counties.** Strong builder activity in $325K to $425K range in west Madison County, Athens (Limestone County), and select Cullman corridors. Builder concessions are common and can be structured to layer with VA's 4% concession cap.
- **Builder concessions VA buyers commonly access.** Closing-cost credits, rate buydowns (subject to the 4% cap when seller-funded), prepaid escrow, optional-feature upgrades within the contract.
- **Seller-paid closing cost prevalence.** Seller-paid termite, seller-paid title, and seller contributions to buyer's closing costs are routine in this market. Sellers and listing agents who treat seller concessions as a deal-breaker filter themselves out of the VA buyer pool.
- **Inventory and days-on-market trends.** Madison County reported 91 days on market in January 2026 (Redfin); Huntsville city reported 83 days (Redfin); Decatur reported 63 days. Days on market are extending versus 2024-early 2025 highs, which gives VA buyers more negotiating room.
- **Rental rates relative to BAH for Redstone.** For E-6 and above with dependents, the BAH-to-mortgage math at Redstone often favors buying over renting in the $300K to $400K price segment, particularly with AHFA Step Up and seller concessions reducing cash-to-close. Specific calculations should be run buyer-by-buyer; do not publish a generic claim that buying is cheaper than renting.
- **The school district premium.** Madison City Schools is consistently rated A or A+ on third-party rankings (Niche.com, etc.) and commands a measurable price premium versus equivalent square footage in Huntsville City Schools or Madison County Schools attendance zones (source: niche.com/k12/search/best-public-schools/t/madison-madison-al/, accessed 05-09-2026; cityfindr.com/huntsville-al/schools-education, accessed 05-09-2026). For VA buyers BAH-constrained at the lower end, the Madison City premium can push a desired square footage out of BAH-comfortable PITI; Madison County Schools (Sparkman zone, etc.) often becomes the budget-fit alternative.
- **Commute patterns to Redstone Gates.** Gate 9 (Rideout Road) is the Madison City and Madison County west-side commuter gate. Gate 7 is the Research Park commuter gate. Gate 1 is the historical main gate from downtown Huntsville. Gate 10 serves the south/Owens Cross Roads side. Commute time from Madison City to Gate 9 in normal traffic runs 15 to 25 minutes; from Hartselle (Morgan County) to Gate 9, 35 to 45 minutes; from Hazel Green (north Madison County) to Gate 9, 30 to 40 minutes; from Athens to Gate 9, 25 to 35 minutes. (Verify with current traffic patterns and any Garrison gate-hour changes; Resolute Way connector to I-565 is in planning to ease congestion.)

# 10. Real Borrower Questions (Local AEO-Targeted)

The questions below are written in the buyer's first-person voice and the buyer's register, not in industry voice. Each is paired with the underlying intent and a precise factual answer suitable for AEO-formatted content.

## Where do most active-duty Army families live around Redstone?

**Intent:** "I'm PCSing in. Where do families like mine actually live?"

**Answer:** Active-duty families assigned to Redstone tend to cluster in three areas. The first is Madison City, west of Redstone, with the highest-rated public schools (Madison City Schools) and a 12 to 18 minute commute to Gate 9. The second is the Hampton Cove and Owens Cross Roads side, southeast of the city, with Huntsville City Schools magnet options and a longer commute through the south side. The third is north Madison County (Harvest, Hazel Green, Meridianville, Toney) for buyers prioritizing larger lots and lower price points within Madison County Schools attendance zones. Smaller numbers also live in Decatur, Athens, and Hartselle for cost-of-living reasons, accepting a 30 to 45 minute commute. The Redstone Sponsor Program through the gaining unit provides current school-and-neighborhood orientation specific to the assignment.

## What's the BAH for Redstone Arsenal?

**Intent:** "How much housing money do I get?"

**Answer:** Redstone Arsenal falls in the Huntsville, AL Military Housing Area (MHA AL003). 2026 BAH for the Huntsville MHA ticked down approximately 0.9% from 2025, decoupling from the broader Alabama statewide increase of approximately 5.4%. Specific 2026 amounts for typical ranks: E-6 with dependents approximately $2,079 per month; O-3 with dependents approximately $2,226 per month. BAH varies by rank and dependent status and changes annually on January 1. Look up the exact current rate at the official DoD BAH Calculator at travel.dod.mil. Lenders often gross-up tax-free BAH for qualifying-income calculations.

## How long does a VA loan take to close in Alabama?

**Intent:** "Is this going to take forever?"

**Answer:** A VA loan in North Alabama closes routinely in 21 to 35 days from a complete contract with a VA-experienced local lender. Speed-of-closing depends on appraisal turnaround (typically 5 to 14 business days from order to report), termite inspection scheduling and any treatment cycle (typically 7 to 14 days end-to-end), title work, and lender-specific underwriting throughput. The "VA loans take forever" reputation is dated; experienced Alabama VA lenders close on conventional-comparable timelines. Out-of-state lenders unfamiliar with attorney-state closings, NPMA-33 dynamics, and AHFA reservation can extend the timeline by a week or more.

## Will sellers accept my VA offer in Madison?

**Intent:** "Am I at a disadvantage because I'm using VA?"

**Answer:** Madison County is a high-VA market because of Redstone. Sellers and listing agents in this market are experienced with VA offers, and VA offers are routinely accepted. The "VA offers don't get accepted" narrative is a national-blog talking point that does not match local reality at typical North Alabama price points. A VA offer with a strong pre-approval letter from a local VA-experienced lender, a 21 to 30 day close timeline, and a competitive price is competitive with conventional. The 4% seller concession structure plus unlimited allowable-closing-cost credits gives the seller room to make the deal work.

## Is Hazel Green / Harvest / Meridianville USDA-eligible for VA buyers?

**Intent:** "Can I use USDA instead of VA, or alongside VA?"

**Answer:** USDA Rural Development and VA are separate programs. USDA-eligibility for a specific address in Hazel Green, Harvest, Meridianville, New Market, Toney, Owens Cross Roads, or other unincorporated Madison County locations depends on the property's USDA-eligibility status, which is determined by census-tract designation. Many addresses in these communities are USDA-eligible, but the eligibility is property-specific. Verify any specific address at https://eligibility.sc.egov.usda.gov/eligibility/welcomeAction.do (USDA Rural Development eligibility map). A VA-eligible buyer can choose between VA and USDA on an eligible property; VA typically wins on borrower flexibility (no income cap on most files, no upfront guarantee fee equivalent to USDA's), but USDA can sometimes win on rate or fee structure. A local lender experienced in both programs can run the comparison.

## Do I need a termite letter for a VA loan in Alabama?

**Intent:** "What's this NPMA-33 thing my lender mentioned?"

**Answer:** Yes, virtually every VA transaction in Alabama includes a Wood Destroying Insect Inspection Report (NPMA-33). Alabama's subterranean termite pressure is high, and the report is routine. The inspection is performed by a state-licensed pest control company. The buyer or lender typically orders it; the seller commonly pays for it under customary contract allocation. If active infestation or visible damage is found, treatment by a licensed company and a clearance letter are required before closing. The report is typically valid for 90 days for underwriting.

## Can I use my VA loan if I'm PCSing to Redstone?

**Intent:** "Does VA work for me at this stage of my career?"

**Answer:** Yes. PCSing to Redstone is one of the most common scenarios VA was designed to serve. The VA owner-occupancy rule allows your spouse to fulfill the occupancy requirement when you are unable to personally occupy because of military duty. The 60-day reasonable-time rule applies, with documented intent to occupy. Active-duty buyers commonly use VA at Redstone whether they have full entitlement (no prior VA loan use, or prior VA loan paid off and entitlement restored) or partial entitlement (keeping a prior-station home as a rental). Start the lender conversation at receipt of orders, not after arrival in Huntsville.

## Can my spouse close on the house before I arrive at Redstone?

**Intent:** "We need to be in the home before I report. Is that OK with VA?"

**Answer:** Yes. The VA owner-occupancy rule is satisfied when the spouse occupies the home on the active-duty service member's behalf during the period the service member cannot personally occupy because of military duty. Both spouses sign loan documents (or a properly executed power of attorney can be used for the deployed or absent service member), and the spouse moves in, satisfying the owner-occupancy expectation. Document intent to occupy on the service member's part, with reporting-date paperwork, and work with a VA-experienced lender to structure the file correctly. Route legal-document execution and POA questions to the closing attorney and JAG.

## What's AHFA Salute Our Soldiers and can I use it with a VA loan?

**Intent:** "I heard about a state veteran program. What is it?"

**Answer:** "Salute Our Soldiers" is a Florida program, not an Alabama program. Alabama's equivalent down payment assistance for VA buyers is the **AHFA Step Up program**, administered by the Alabama Housing Finance Authority. Step Up provides up to $10,000 or 4% of the purchase price (whichever is less) as a 10-year second mortgage paired with a 30-year fixed-rate first mortgage. It now offers a VA-paired first mortgage option in addition to FHA and conventional. Income limit is $159,200 regardless of household size or location; minimum credit score 640; debt-to-income maximum 45%; homebuyer education course required. The MCC (Mortgage Credit Certificate) and First Step are companion AHFA programs that can layer in. Apply through an AHFA-participating lender, not directly with AHFA.

## How does the Alabama homestead exemption work for active-duty?

**Intent:** "I'm active-duty. Will the homestead exemption apply to me?"

**Answer:** The Alabama homestead exemption applies to property owners who own and occupy a single-family residence as their primary residence on October 1 of the tax year. Active-duty service members who own and occupy a Madison County (or other North Alabama county) home as their primary residence qualify on the same terms as civilian owners. The deadline to apply is December 31 of the tax year. Filing requires a copy of the recorded deed, an Alabama driver's license matching the property address, and presentation at the county Tax Assessor or Revenue Commissioner office. Specific SCRA-related property-tax considerations for service members domiciled in another state are fact-specific; route those questions to JAG, the county Revenue Commissioner, or military legal assistance.

## Can I keep my house in [prior duty station] and buy a new one with VA at Redstone?

**Intent:** "I'm PCSing but I want to keep the prior house as a rental."

**Answer:** Yes, in many cases. This is the second-tier (bonus) entitlement scenario. You can retain your prior-station VA loan and use remaining VA entitlement for a new VA loan at Redstone, subject to the conforming loan limit math. With Madison County's standard $832,750 conforming loan limit and a typical Redstone-area purchase below that level, the math frequently allows zero-down at Redstone even when prior entitlement is partially used. The exact remaining entitlement and the maximum zero-down purchase price depend on the prior loan amount and the COE entitlement math (mechanics file). Run this with a VA-experienced lender at receipt of orders; do not assume.

## I'm retiring at Redstone. Should I use VA now or wait until I'm out?

**Intent:** "What's the funding-fee, disability-rating, and entitlement timing question?"

**Answer:** This depends on whether you have a documented disability rating (or a pending claim), your retirement timeline, and your post-retirement income picture. Two specific considerations: (1) The VA funding fee is exempt for veterans receiving compensation for a service-connected disability. If you have a pending claim that is likely to be approved with an effective date before your loan-closing date, you may be eligible for a funding fee refund after closing. (2) The VA retirement-occupancy exception allows a buyer to delay personal occupancy by up to 12 months when retirement-into-the-property is documented and post-retirement income is verified. For specific timing decisions, route the disability-rating question to VA and the funding-fee-versus-wait analysis to a VA-experienced lender. **Do not take disability-claim or VA-rating advice from a Realtor or lender.** Consult a VA-accredited representative or county Veterans Service Officer.

## Do disabled veterans pay the VA funding fee in Alabama?

**Intent:** "Am I exempt from the funding fee?"

**Answer:** The VA funding fee is exempt for veterans receiving VA compensation for a service-connected disability, veterans eligible to receive compensation but receiving retirement or active-duty pay instead, surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability, service members with proposed or memorandum ratings before loan closing for pre-discharge claims, and active-duty Purple Heart recipients. The exemption applies in Alabama on the same terms as anywhere else; this is federal rule. Mechanics file has the full exemption framework. Route specific eligibility questions to VA, never to the lender or Realtor as the final word.

## What schools are best for military families near Redstone?

**Intent:** "Where do I send my kids?"

**Answer:** Three public school districts serve the Redstone area: Madison City Schools, Huntsville City Schools, and Madison County Schools. Madison City Schools is consistently rated among the top public school districts in Alabama on third-party rankings and serves much of the area immediately west of Redstone. Huntsville City Schools is large and diverse with several highly-rated magnet programs (New Century Technology High School, others). Madison County Schools serves Harvest, Meridianville, and surrounding areas in the Sparkman attendance zone. Limestone County Schools serves Athens and surrounding Limestone County. The Redstone School Liaison Office can help families match an address to the correct district and navigate enrollment, especially for mid-year transfers. Contact through the gaining unit's Sponsor Program.

## How much is homeowner's insurance in Huntsville for a $400K home?

**Intent:** "What's my insurance premium going to be?"

**Answer:** Homeowner's insurance in Huntsville for a $300,000 to $400,000 home commonly runs $1,500 to $3,000 per year, with the most cited current averages around $2,300 to $2,900 per year. Premiums vary widely by carrier, roof age, claims history, deductible, and Fortified status. North Alabama is a tornado and severe-storm market, not a hurricane market, so premiums are below Gulf Coast Alabama levels. The Strengthen Alabama Homes program offers grants up to $10,000 for FORTIFIED roof retrofits, which can earn wind-portion premium discounts of up to 35% to 55% depending on carrier. Get quotes from at least three carriers; do not assume a single quoted premium is representative.

## Can a Reservist drilling at the Decatur armory get a VA loan?

**Intent:** "I'm Reserve. Do I qualify?"

**Answer:** Yes, if you meet the federal-rule qualifying-service thresholds for Reserve VA eligibility. Mechanics file has the full eligibility framework. Reservists drilling at the Decatur armory (or any other North Alabama armory) qualify on the same terms as Reservists nationally. The most common documentation differences from active-duty borrowers are: (1) the lender may need a Statement of Service or NGB-22 documenting qualifying service; (2) lenders less experienced with Reserve files may produce a hesitant initial answer that is not actually a denial. Work with a lender experienced in Reserve VA files. Route eligibility-determination questions to the St. Petersburg VA Regional Loan Center or to the Madison County Veterans Service Officer.
