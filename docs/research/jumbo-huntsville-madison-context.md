## 2. Plain-English Summary (450 words)

Madison County, Alabama, sits at the FHFA baseline conforming loan limit, currently $832,750 for one-unit properties (effective 01-01-2026). None of the seven North Alabama counties this packet covers (Madison, Limestone, Morgan, Marshall, Jackson, DeKalb, Cullman) carry a high-cost designation, so the conforming-to-jumbo line is exactly the same in every one of them. That single fact reshapes how jumbo lending behaves in this market and is the most important local detail a buyer needs to absorb before anything else.

Most national jumbo content describes a market where significant percentages of home sales clear the conforming threshold, where pools of qualified jumbo-experienced appraisers are deep, where comp data is plentiful, and where local lenders have non-stop reps in the segment. North Alabama is not that market. The Huntsville-area median sale price hovered between roughly $315,000 and $345,000 across most of 2025 (HAAR, Matt Curtis, Redfin), well below the conforming line. Jumbo activity is concentrated in a comparatively small set of submarkets (Hampton Cove, McMullen Cove, Twickenham, Monte Sano, Madison's Clift's Cove, the lake markets, custom-build estates, and a thin band of luxury infill), and that concentration creates the local dynamics this packet documents.

The buyer base is also distinct. The defense, aerospace, and federal sector employs more than 70,000 people regionally and contributes more than $6 billion in annual economic impact, and Redstone Arsenal alone supports about 95,000 jobs and roughly $36 billion in statewide economic impact (Huntsville-Madison County Chamber, Redstone Garrison). Senior contractors with RSU and PSU compensation, NASA Marshall and federal SES leaders, transitioning senior military officers, Huntsville Hospital and Madison Hospital physicians, and inbound relocation buyers from significantly higher-cost markets together drive most of the local jumbo flow. Each of those buyer types has documentation and qualification realities that the universal mechanics file does not address in local terms.

The local realities that most reshape universal jumbo mechanics in North Alabama are: a thin local jumbo-experienced appraiser pool with comp scarcity in several flagship submarkets; a tornado and severe-storm exposure profile that affects insurance pricing, deductibles, and FORTIFIED-related product decisions; the lowest effective property tax in the country (Alabama state-level effective rate near 0.37 to 0.56% in Madison County depending on jurisdiction) which becomes a meaningful planning lever at jumbo prices; an Alabama Housing Finance Authority program landscape that sits well below jumbo price points; a post-2019 VA full-entitlement environment that lets qualified veterans buy at jumbo amounts with no down payment; an attorney-state closing process that surprises buyers from non-attorney states; and a relocation buyer pattern bringing higher-cost-market expectations and equity into a market with a tighter local jumbo ecosystem than they assume. This packet documents each of those.

## 3. The Madison County Conforming Threshold and Local Market Reality

### 3.1 The Threshold in This Market

The 2026 baseline FHFA conforming loan limit for one-unit properties is $832,750, an increase of $26,250 from the 2025 baseline of $806,500. The increase reflects a 3.26% year-over-year change in the FHFA seasonally adjusted, expanded-data House Price Index between Q3 2024 and Q3 2025 (FHFA, "FHFA Announces Conforming Loan Limit Values for 2026," 11-25-2025: https://www.fhfa.gov/news/news-release/fhfa-announces-conforming-loan-limit-values-for-2026, accessed 05-09-2026).

The 2026 high-cost-area ceiling for one-unit properties is $1,249,125 (150% of the baseline). Special statutory provisions place Alaska, Hawaii, Guam, and the U.S. Virgin Islands at the ceiling-as-floor of $1,249,125 (FHFA same release).

The states with high-cost area designations in 2026 are: California, Colorado, Connecticut, District of Columbia, Florida, Hawaii, Idaho, Maryland, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New York, Pennsylvania, Tennessee, Utah, Virginia, Washington, West Virginia, and Wyoming (Fannie Mae, "Loan Limits": https://singlefamily.fanniemae.com/originating-underwriting/loan-limits, accessed 05-09-2026). **Alabama is not on the list.** Every Alabama county sits at the baseline.

That means in Madison, Limestone, Morgan, Marshall, Jackson, DeKalb, and Cullman counties, the 2026 one-unit conforming loan limit is $832,750. The 2026 multi-unit limits in these counties are $1,066,000 (two-unit), $1,288,875 (three-unit), and $1,601,450 (four-unit) (FHFA county lookup table referenced through https://mortgage-info.com/blog/conforming-loan-limits-2026-by-state-county-lookup, accessed 05-09-2026).

The practical upshot for buyers and writers: there is no "Madison County is high-cost" angle to play, no escape valve into an in-between high-balance product, and no local-area enhancement to the limit. The conforming-to-jumbo line in this market is one number, $832,750, for one-unit properties.

### 3.2 What Percentage of the Market Crosses Into Jumbo

Public market summaries from the Huntsville Area Association of Realtors and Valley MLS do not break out loan-amount distributions, but the price-distribution data lets us estimate jumbo penetration as a share of single-family transactions in Madison and Limestone counties.

The Madison County year-over-year median sale price (Huntsville-Madison MLS submarket) ran roughly $315,000 to $345,000 across most of 2025 (HAAR weekly and monthly reports, summarized by Matt Curtis Real Estate market reports, "Huntsville, AL Housing Market Report" series 03-2025 through 09-2025: https://www.mattcurtisrealestate.com/HuntsvilleAlRealEstateBlog/, accessed 05-09-2026; Redfin Huntsville market summary, accessed 05-09-2026: https://www.redfin.com/city/9408/AL/Huntsville/housing-market). The September 2025 Madison County median was $320,000 against $338,000 the prior year (Matt Curtis, "Huntsville AL Housing Market Report September 2025," accessed 05-09-2026).

The implied jumbo-side share is small in absolute terms. The vast majority of Madison County single-family transactions clear well below the $832,750 conforming line. The submarket concentration of jumbo activity is what matters for jumbo planning, not the average across all transactions.

**Open verification request for the writer:** HAAR or Valley MLS internal price-tier data (transactions sold above $832,750, $1M, $1.5M) is the cleanest way to size the jumbo segment precisely. The writer should request a tier breakdown directly from HAAR or pull it from the MLS. This is important enough that the page should not assert percentages without sourced numbers.

The price-distribution shifts that drove "more buyers into jumbo territory" since 2020 include:

- Average Huntsville sale prices roughly doubled from pre-pandemic levels into the high $300,000s, with luxury submarkets running at corresponding multiples. Pre-pandemic Huntsville average sale prices were in the high $180,000s (Matt Curtis Real Estate, "May 2025 Market Report").
- New construction in luxury submarkets has pushed median prices in those submarkets above the conforming line where they were not before. Hampton Cove's 12-month median sat at roughly $430,000 to $440,000 in late 2025 and early 2026 (Homes.com Hampton Cove neighborhood summary, accessed 05-09-2026: https://www.homes.com/hampton-cove-al/), but new-construction premium transactions in McMullen Cove, Stoneybrook, and the eastern-Madison-County custom builds run materially higher.
- The Twickenham Historic District 12-month median sat at $1,070,000 (Homes.com Twickenham summary, accessed 05-09-2026: https://www.homes.com/huntsville-al/twickenham-historic-district-neighborhood/), up 10% year over year. Every Twickenham single-family transaction at or near that median is a jumbo transaction.

### 3.3 Why Jumbo Planning Matters More Now

The structural drivers pushing more buyers into jumbo territory in this market:

- **Defense and aerospace senior compensation growth.** The defense and aerospace industry employs more than 70,000 workers regionally and contributes over $6 billion in annual economic impact (Huntsville-Madison County Chamber, cited in Huntsville Business Journal "Golden Dome" coverage 09-01-2025: https://huntsvillebusinessjournal.com/news/2025/09/01/huntsvilles-golden-dome-what-we-know-now-and-whats-coming-next/, accessed 05-09-2026). Senior contractors with RSU and PSU stock compensation are a structural part of the buyer base for higher-priced submarkets.
- **The Space Command relocation.** The phased relocation of U.S. Space Command headquarters to Redstone Arsenal is bringing approximately 1,400 employees over a five-year window (Business Alabama, "Huntsville's space legacy enters a new era," 12-05-2025: https://businessalabama.com/huntsvilles-space-legacy-enters-a-new-era/, accessed 05-09-2026). Senior-officer and senior-civilian relocations within that group are inbound to a market where they expect higher-priced submarkets and where some are bringing equity from substantially higher-cost markets.
- **Inbound relocation from high-cost markets.** Bay Area, Seattle, DC metro, NYC, Boston, Southern California, Atlanta, Austin, and Denver buyers continue to inbound for defense, aerospace, biotech, and federal positions. Their starting equity from a sale in those markets often clears half a million dollars or more, and they shop accordingly.
- **New construction in luxury submarkets.** Custom and semi-custom new construction in Hampton Cove, McMullen Cove, the Ledges, Stoneybrook, Heritage Plantation, Owens Cross Roads, Big Cove, and the Madison luxury subdivisions consistently delivers homes priced above the conforming threshold.
- **Lake home market appreciation.** Smith Lake waterfront ran an average sold price of about $996,306 in Q1 2025, with active list averages of about $1,161,482 (Lake & Coast Real Estate Co., "Smith Lake Waterfront Market Report April 2025": https://lakeandcoast.com/smith-lake-waterfront-homes-market-report-april-2025/, accessed 05-09-2026). Lake Guntersville carries a typical waterfront median list price near $649,000 (Redfin Guntersville waterfront, accessed 05-09-2026: https://www.redfin.com/city/8275/AL/Guntersville/waterfront), with the high end of the Guntersville luxury market running well above the conforming line.

## 4. Higher-Priced Submarkets and Neighborhoods

This section compiles factual market data only. Language drift toward "best," "most desirable," "exclusive," or "the right kind of community" must be flagged and rewritten by the writer per HUD FHEO and NAR guidance covered in Section 17.

### 4.1 Hampton Cove and Eastern Madison County

Hampton Cove is a master-planned community on the eastern side of Huntsville, comprised of approximately 19 to 20 individual subdivisions across roughly 2,800 acres at the foot of Monte Sano Mountain. The community was established beginning in 1992 (Movoto Hampton Cove neighborhood guide, accessed 05-09-2026: https://www.movoto.com/guide/huntsville-al/hampton-cove-real-estate-huntsville-al-neighborhood-guide/). Hampton Cove sits within Huntsville city limits and is served by Huntsville City Schools at the elementary and middle level, with attendance at Huntsville High School.

Submarket reference points (sources cited):

- **Hampton Cove proper**: 12-month median sale price approximately $430,000 to $440,000 (Homes.com Hampton Cove summary, accessed 05-09-2026: https://www.homes.com/hampton-cove-al/). Note that this aggregate median includes the broader Hampton Cove footprint. Specific subdivisions inside Hampton Cove run materially above this.
- **McMullen Cove**: A higher-end custom-build subdivision within Hampton Cove. Lot prices and custom-home transactions regularly clear the conforming threshold. The writer should pull an MLS-restricted subdivision-only median rather than rely on the aggregate Hampton Cove number.
- **The Ledges** (private golf community): Membership-restricted residential community. Per Garrison Commander Col. Eike's NASA Marshall retirees luncheon held there 03-10-2026, The Ledges hosts community-scale events; transaction-level data require MLS access. Comp depth is materially thinner than typical Hampton Cove.
- **Stoneybrook**: Custom-build subdivision adjacent to Hampton Cove infrastructure.
- **Big Cove**: Larger-lot, lower-density area east of Hampton Cove proper.
- **Heritage Plantation**: Located in Owens Cross Roads (the writer should verify the exact address range; the community straddles the Hampton Cove-Owens Cross Roads boundary). Larger lots and custom builds.
- **Owens Cross Roads luxury**: Estate-acreage and custom-build inventory east of the Hampton Cove footprint.
- **Goldsmith-Schiffman elementary attendance zone properties**: Attendance-zone-anchored higher-priced inventory.

### 4.2 South Huntsville

- **Blossomwood**: Older established luxury and near-luxury inventory adjacent to the Twickenham, Five Points, and downtown corridor. Blossomwood Elementary is in the Huntsville City Schools attendance zone.
- **Jones Valley**: Older established higher-priced inventory south of downtown.
- **Monte Sano**: Mountain-estate inventory with view premium and acreage; comp scarcity is the dominant valuation challenge here.
- **Green Mountain**: Mountain-estate inventory with view premium and acreage. The June 7, 2025 storm event in this area is documented at https://hailtrace.com/hail-maps/06-07-2025/al/huntsville (accessed 05-09-2026).

### 4.3 Downtown and Central Huntsville

- **Twickenham (historic district)**: The city's first officially designated historic district, established 1972. 12-month median sale price approximately $1,070,000 with year-over-year median up 10% (Homes.com Twickenham, accessed 05-09-2026: https://www.homes.com/huntsville-al/twickenham-historic-district-neighborhood/). Average days on market approximately 95 days against a national average of 55 days. Twickenham is zoned to Huntsville City Schools (Blossomwood Elementary, Huntsville Junior High, Huntsville High School). Inventory is grand pre-Civil War estates, Greek Revival mansions, Queen Anne homes, and Craftsman cottages, and homeownership in the district is described as predominantly multi-generational with relatively limited turnover.
- **Five Points**: Adjacent historic district, smaller cottage and Craftsman inventory at lower price points than Twickenham.
- **Old Town**: Historic core inventory.
- **The Avenues / downtown urban infill**: Newer luxury condominium product (e.g., the Annie Merts conversion of an old red-brick high school into one- to three-bedroom condos listing between $425,000 and $1,000,000 per Homes.com summary, accessed 05-09-2026). 301 East and similar mid-rise condos are part of this segment.

### 4.4 Madison

- **Clift's Cove**: Executive-planned community at the southwest side of Rainbow Mountain in Madison city limits. Homes range from approximately $500,000 to multi-million-dollar estates, with 2025 active inventory listing $489,000 to $1,899,000 and individual listings at $1,295,000, $1,575,000, and $1,875,000+ (sourced from Cameron Walker Realty Clift's Cove summary, accessed 05-09-2026: https://www.cameronwalkerrealty.com/madison/clifts-cove/, and from @Homes Realty Group Clift's Cove subdivision summary, accessed 05-09-2026: https://athomeshuntsville.com/homesforsale/index.php?action=searchresults&subdivision=clifts+cove&city=madison). Mandatory HOA approximately $1,000 annually. Properties are primarily in Flood Zone X (minimal hazard).
- **Westchester**: Madison upper-tier subdivision; verify current median with MLS.
- **Madison Lakes**: Lake-themed community with above-average price points.
- **The Cove at Plantation Lakes**: Verify activity with MLS.
- **Old Towne Madison**: Historic core inventory at lower price points than Clift's Cove.
- **Town Madison**: Mixed-use development surrounding the Toyota Field stadium and Trash Pandas baseball venue. Higher-density product with luxury townhome and condo inventory.
- **Heritage of Madison**: Verify with MLS.
- **Open verification request**: The writer should request a current Madison luxury subdivision ranking by 12-month median from a Madison-specialist Realtor and from Valley MLS.

### 4.7 Lake and Recreational Markets

- **Smith Lake** (Cullman, Walker, Winston counties): Approximately 500 miles of shoreline and 21,000-acre lake; the second-largest lake home market in Alabama by inventory. Q1 2025 average waterfront active list price approximately $1,161,482 and average sold price approximately $996,306. Full-year 2024 average waterfront sold price was approximately $878,761 across 210 sales and 267 new listings, with median lake-home price reported near $1,189,280 and average lot prices near $350,544 (Jay Sells Jasper Smith Lake summary citing Lake & Coast Real Estate data, accessed 05-09-2026: https://jaysellsjasper.com/neighborhoods/smith-lake; Lake & Coast Smith Lake Waterfront Market Report April 2025: https://lakeandcoast.com/smith-lake-waterfront-homes-market-report-april-2025/).
- **Lake Guntersville** (Marshall and Jackson counties): Alabama's largest lake at approximately 69,100 acres and 962 miles of shoreline. ## 6. The Relocation Buyer Reality

This is the highest-leverage local content area. Inbound relocation buyers are over-represented in the local jumbo segment, and most national jumbo content does not address the relocation realities specific to a market that combines a thin local jumbo ecosystem with a high inbound flow from substantially higher-cost markets.

### 6.1 Corporate Relocation Package Structures

The relocation industry's standardized program types (per WERC, the rebranded Worldwide ERC, https://www.worldwideerc.org/, accessed 05-09-2026; and per industry explainers from NRI Relocation, Aires, WHR Global, and InterLink Relocation Resources):

- **Full-service relocation programs (managed by an RMC, "Relocation Management Company")**: The RMC handles household goods move, temporary housing, destination services, closing-cost coverage on the buy-side, and home-sale assistance on the sell-side.
- **Lump-sum relocation programs**: The employer transfers a pre-negotiated dollar amount to the employee, and the employee handles logistics. Common at smaller employers and at junior levels; rare at senior levels relevant to the local jumbo market.
- **Hybrid programs**: Lump sum for some categories (move, temporary housing) and managed services for others (closing-cost coverage, home-sale assistance).
- **Buyer Value Option (BVO) home-sale program**: The employee lists the departing residence and secures an outside buyer; the RMC purchases the home from the employee at the outside-buyer offer price and resells to the outside buyer in a separate transaction. The two-sale structure delivers tax-protected home-sale treatment for the employee.
- **Guaranteed Buyout (GBO) / Amended Value Option (AVO) home-sale program**: The RMC commits to purchase the home at a guaranteed value (typically derived from two Worldwide ERC Relocation Appraisals averaged), sets a 60- to 120-day marketing period, and if an outside buyer arrives at a higher number the price is amended (AVO). GBO/AVO is typically reserved for VP, executive, and C-suite relocations because the employer assumes more inventory risk.

What relocation packages typically cover for senior relocations: household goods move, temporary housing (typically 30 to 90 days), buy-side closing costs, destination services (school referrals, area orientation), and gross-up on taxable benefit categories.

What relocation packages typically do not cover: mortgage rate buy-down beyond the standard rate-buy-down points covered as closing costs; jumbo investor placement (the RMC does not pick the lender's overlay); appraisal-gap shortfalls on the buy-side (if the buy-side appraisal comes in below contract, the relocation package does not bridge that gap); and discretionary lender-fee differences between the relocation provider's preferred lender and a buyer-chosen alternative.

### 6.2 Concurrent Closing Mechanics

The structural challenge: selling in California, Washington, Texas, DC metro, or another non-attorney state and buying in Alabama (an attorney state) on parallel timelines is materially harder than selling and buying in the same state.

- The seller-side state may use escrow companies, title companies, or attorneys depending on the state. The buyer-side Alabama transaction is closed through a real estate attorney, with the attorney coordinating disbursements.
- Bridge loans and HELOC products on the departing residence are common gap-financing tools. The universal mechanics file should cover the underwriting framework. Locally, BVO/GBO buyout proceeds may not arrive in the buyer's account by the buy-side closing date, and using "expected" buyout proceeds as the source of cash to close is a documentation risk.
- Relocation buyout proceeds count as documented cash to close once received and seasoned. Until received, they are an expectation, not a documented asset.
- Closing attorney coordination across states should begin at least 30 days before the buy-side close and ideally 45 to 60 days. The Alabama-side attorney will be working with title-company or escrow personnel in the prior state, and timing of wire arrival is the most common point of friction.

**Route concurrent-close legal coordination to the closing attorneys in both states. Loan officer is not the right party for legal coordination.**

### 6.3 Tax and Financial Planning Considerations

Relocation overlays on tax planning include:

- Capital gains on the departing-residence sale (Section 121 primary-residence exclusion of up to $250,000 single / $500,000 married applies if the use and ownership tests are met; consult the IRS and a CPA before assuming).
- State residency timing on dual-tax-home situations during the relocation window.
- RSU vesting that overlaps the relocation; vesting in one state and selling shares in another can produce dual-state tax exposure.
- Stock option exercise timing in the relocation window.

**Route every tax question to a CPA, ideally one experienced with multi-state relocation.** The loan officer is not authorized to provide tax advice.

### 6.4 Timeline Mismatches

Common mismatch:

- Relocation provider's expectation: 30 days from contract to close.
- Realistic local jumbo timeline: 35 to 45 days, sometimes longer if a two-appraisal trigger fires (see Section 7) or if the borrower's income documentation requires manual underwriter work.
- Realistic VA jumbo timeline: 35 to 50 days, with VA appraisal timing variable by appraiser-pool depth in the submarket.

The buyer who plans on a 30-day close in this market is setting up for either an unsuccessful close or a heavily compressed underwriting and appraisal window. Loan officer-led timeline planning at the start of the relocation conversation is the single most leveraged intervention.

### 6.5 Common Relocation-Buyer Pitfalls

- Underestimating cash-to-close on the buy-side because BVO/GBO proceeds have not arrived by the closing date.
- Assuming the relocation provider's recommended lender is the best fit for jumbo. The relocation provider's lender list is a curated list, often weighted toward national-brand lenders with relocation-program experience but not necessarily the strongest local jumbo overlay.
- Bringing higher-cost-market service expectations and underestimating a local market that runs at a different pace.
- Underestimating Alabama-specific transaction realities including the termite letter (a standard contract item in Alabama that buyers from non-termite-letter states do not anticipate), the attorney-state closing process, and the homestead-exemption application requirement to capture the favorable property tax treatment (see Section 11).
- Bringing higher-cost-market price expectations and overshooting affordability locally (the inverse: bringing higher-cost-market price expectations to a market where the same dollar buys more home, but the property carries higher utility, insurance, and maintenance liability than the prior market).
- Bringing lower-cost-market price expectations and undershooting school-zone-anchored realities (a buyer relocating in from a smaller market may not have priced the school-zone premium that some Madison City and Hampton Cove zones carry).

## 7. Local Appraisal Dynamics for Jumbo

### 7.1 Local Appraiser Pool

The qualified jumbo-experienced appraiser pool in North Alabama is materially smaller than in coastal high-cost markets. Universal jumbo investor frameworks that assume deep appraiser pools must be adjusted for this market.

Realities:

- AMC (Appraisal Management Company) assignment dynamics often deliver appraisers from outside the immediate market on jumbo files. Out-of-area appraiser assignments are a structural risk in submarkets with thin comp pools (Hampton Cove, McMullen Cove, Twickenham, Monte Sano, Green Mountain, the Ledges, Smith Lake, Lake Guntersville luxury).
- Local-lender choices often correlate with stronger local appraiser assignment because local lenders maintain local appraiser networks that AMCs assigning out-of-area appraisers do not.
- The writer should not assert specific appraiser counts without sourced data; verification request: ask the local Realtor and lender community for the count of appraisers actively closing jumbo files in Madison and Limestone counties.

### 7.2 Comp Scarcity in Luxury Submarkets

- **Hampton Cove and McMullen Cove**: Comp pool depth is moderate at the $500K to $750K level and thinner above. New-construction comps in McMullen Cove are most exposed because the new-build pool may not have closed enough recent comps to support the sale price.
- **Twickenham historic district**: The lot uniqueness, view premium, and pre-Civil-War era construction make comp-grade matching challenging. Few houses in Twickenham are truly directly comparable. The 12-month transaction count in Twickenham is small.
- **Monte Sano and Green Mountain**: Mountain-estate inventory with view premium and acreage. Acreage adjustments and view-premium adjustments are not standardized; appraiser choice matters here.
- **The Ledges**: Private-golf-community comp challenge; small transaction count; the writer should not name absolute counts without sourced data.
- **Hampton Cove subdivisions newer than 2018**: New-build maturation; comps mature over time as recent sales accumulate.
- **Smith Lake luxury and Lake Guntersville luxury**: Waterfront-comp scarcity is structural. Depth of water, length of shoreline, dock infrastructure, and exposure (point lot vs. cove) all materially affect value, and the appraiser must be familiar with these adjustments.

### 7.3 The Two-Appraisal Trigger in This Market

Universal jumbo investors require two appraisals on certain higher-value transactions, typically at $1.5M+ or $2M+ depending on the investor (per industry summaries: https://aaacapitalfunding.com/mortgage-loan-programs/jumbo-loans/, accessed 05-09-2026; https://www.lendingtree.com/home/mortgage/what-is-a-jumbo-loan/, accessed 05-09-2026). When a two-appraisal trigger fires:

- The lender will use the lower of the two values.
- Each appraisal carries a separate appraisal fee paid by the buyer.
- Timeline extends, typically by a week to ten days.
- In a thin appraiser pool, ordering two genuinely independent qualified appraisers can be the rate-limiting step.

In North Alabama, the two-appraisal trigger most commonly fires on:

- Twickenham Historic District transactions over $1.2M
- Smith Lake luxury and Lake Guntersville luxury waterfront transactions over $1.5M
- Hampton Cove and McMullen Cove custom builds at the upper end
- Higher-acreage estate properties in Monte Sano, Green Mountain, and Owens Cross Roads at the upper end

### 7.4 Reconsideration of Value (ROV) in This Market

When an appraisal comes in below contract, the Reconsideration of Value (ROV) request is the procedural tool to surface additional comps for the appraiser's review. Local realities:

- Local listing-agent and buyer-agent practice varies on comp support. The most experienced luxury Realtors in the Hampton Cove, Twickenham, and Madison luxury segments will provide a competing-comp packet at the time of the contract.
- Successful ROV scenarios in the local market typically involve sourced sales the original appraiser did not include, paired with an explanation of why those sales are more comparable (proximity, lot size, condition, recency).
- The buyer's agent's role in providing competing comps is usually the most leveraged ROV intervention.

## 8. Local Builder Market in Higher-Priced Segments

### 8.1 Builders Active in Jumbo Price Ranges

Production builders active in the mid-to-upper segments include Davidson Homes, Stoneridge Homes, John Boswell Homes (custom and semi-custom), and Legacy Homebuilders. Custom builders active in Hampton Cove, McMullen Cove, Stoneybrook, Heritage Plantation, and the Madison luxury subdivisions are a fragmented set; the writer should pull a current builder roster from the local Realtor community and from Hampton Cove HOA directly.

**Verification request**: The writer should request a current list of custom builders active in jumbo price ranges (build cost above $300/sq ft) from a local Realtor who specializes in Hampton Cove, McMullen Cove, and Madison luxury. The list rotates as builders enter and exit the market.

### 8.2 Builder Concessions and Incentives

- Lot premiums for view, water, point, and golf-frontage lots are standard. Builders often quote a base lot price and add named premiums.
- Design-center upgrade incentives are common, often delivered as an upgrade allowance ("$10,000 toward design center upgrades") rather than a price reduction.
- Closing-cost contributions and rate buy-downs are common, particularly when paired with the builder's preferred lender. Universal jumbo investor seller-concession caps apply (typically 3% to 6% of purchase price depending on LTV; consult the universal mechanics file).
- Forward commitments (where the builder buys down the rate using a lender-paid mechanism) are common in production-builder segments and rarer in custom segments.
- The trade-off between builder's preferred lender and outside lender pricing is structural: the builder's preferred lender often offers the rate buy-down or closing-cost concession only when the buyer uses that lender, which can lock the buyer out of better outside-lender pricing on the loan itself.

### 8.3 Construction-to-Permanent Jumbo

- The two-time-close structure (a construction loan followed by a separate permanent jumbo at completion) carries two sets of closing costs but allows independent shopping of the permanent loan at completion.
- The one-time-close structure (a single closing locks the construction draw schedule and the permanent loan terms upfront) carries one set of closing costs but requires the rate locked at construction-period start to absorb construction-period interest-rate movement.
- Builder draw schedules and lender coordination matter. Each draw requires inspection-based release, and a delay in the draw process delays the build.
- Appraisal at completion versus subject-to-completion: most one-time-close jumbo programs use a subject-to-completion appraisal at the start with a final completion-condition certificate at the end. Two-time-close structures use a fresh appraisal at the permanent-loan stage, which gives a fresher value but adds a second appraisal cost.

### 8.4 Working with the Builder's Preferred Lender

- When the builder's lender is the right fit: the builder's preferred lender has experience with the specific draw schedule, has handled the builder's prior closings, and the rate-buy-down or concession economics outweigh the spread to outside lender pricing.
- When the builder's lender is not the right fit: when outside lender pricing on rate is meaningfully better, when the buyer's compensation structure or asset structure requires a lender with stronger jumbo overlays, when the buyer wants to use a physician program or a relocation program the builder's lender does not offer, or when the buyer is using a VA full-entitlement strategy that the builder's lender does not specialize in.
- RESPA Section 8 considerations apply when a builder steers buyers to an affiliated lender. Affiliated business arrangement disclosures must be provided. The buyer is not required to use the affiliated lender even if a closing-cost incentive is offered. Verify the disclosure was provided and signed.

### 9.2 Lake Guntersville Luxury

Lake Guntersville is the largest lake in Alabama at approximately 69,100 acres, 962 miles of shoreline. Approximately 200 lake homes typical active inventory. Realities:

- Fluctuating water levels: TVA manages Lake Guntersville at a summer pool elevation of approximately 595 feet and a winter pool elevation of approximately 593 feet. Dock infrastructure must accommodate the seasonal differential. Buyers from non-TVA-managed lake markets do not always anticipate the seasonal pool change.

### 9.3 Tennessee River Frontage

Tennessee River frontage exists in Madison, Limestone, Jackson, Morgan, and Marshall counties. River frontage outside the Wheeler Lake and Lake Guntersville impoundments has different shoreline-management considerations than the impoundment lakes; verify TVA classification and shoreline status.

### 9.4 Second Home vs Primary vs Investment Property Qualification

Cross-reference the universal mechanics file. In summary:

- Second-home jumbo carries different reserve requirements (typically 6 to 12 months of PITI versus 3 to 6 months for primary), DTI ceilings (often tighter), and rate structure (typically 12.5 to 25 basis points higher than primary at otherwise equivalent terms; investor-specific).
- Investment-property jumbo, including DSCR-based products, is a separate underwriting framework; cross-reference the DSCR research file if applicable.
- Mixed-use considerations: a primary residence in Huntsville plus a second home on Smith Lake or Lake Guntersville is a common structure. Both loans coexist; the borrower's DTI must support both PITIs.

### 9.5 Insurance for Lake Properties

- Water damage from lake flooding versus burst-pipe water damage is treated differently by carriers. Flood coverage, where applicable, is via the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or the private flood market.
- Dock and boathouse coverage typically requires a scheduled endorsement on the homeowners policy.
- Liability exposure around recreational use, personal watercraft, and dock-based water entry is materially higher than non-lake properties.
- Higher umbrella policy norms apply on lake properties: a $1M umbrella minimum is commonly recommended for lake-luxury borrowers, though norms vary by carrier and net worth.

## 10. Insurance Considerations at Higher Price Points

### 10.1 Replacement Cost on Luxury Construction

The gap between purchase price and replacement cost on custom homes can be significant in either direction. Custom-build homes in Hampton Cove, McMullen Cove, Stoneybrook, Madison luxury, and downtown infill often carry replacement costs that diverge from purchase price because of:

- Custom finishes, millwork, and specialty interior work not captured in standard replacement-cost calculators.
- Architectural detail that requires specialty labor to rebuild after a loss.
- Site-specific features (terraced lots, structural foundation work, retaining structures) that are not reproduced through a generic rebuild.

Insurance carrier scrutiny of replacement-cost calculations on jumbo-priced custom builds is high. Underinsurance at jumbo loan amounts can trigger lender insurance-binder issues at closing.

### 10.2 Tornado and Severe Storm Exposure

North Alabama sits in the Dixie Alley tornado corridor. The May 18 to 21, 2025 tornado outbreak included an EF2 tornado near Madison and Athens with a Tornado Emergency declared affecting nearly 500,000 people (Wikipedia, "Tornado Outbreak of May 18 to 21, 2025": https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tornado_outbreak_of_May_18%E2%80%9321,_2025, accessed 05-09-2026; Watchers, "Large tornado near Athens," 05-21-2025: https://watchers.news/2025/05/21/large-tornado-athens-alabama-tornado-emergency/, accessed 05-09-2026). Madison County's natural-hazard profile is documented at https://madisoncountyalema.gov/natural-hazards/, with severe thunderstorms identified as the most prevalent local hazard (accessed 05-09-2026).

Insurance implications at jumbo loan amounts:

### 10.3 Older Luxury and Ordinance and Law Coverage

Twickenham Historic District homes pre-date current building codes by decades or in some cases more than a century. Blossomwood and Five Points include similar older inventory.

- Cost-to-rebuild-to-current-code can materially exceed cost-to-rebuild-as-was. Standard homeowners coverage often does not cover the code-upgrade differential.
- Ordinance and Law coverage as a critical line item: typically 10%, 25%, or 50% of dwelling coverage. At jumbo loan amounts, the differential is meaningful.

### 10.4 Umbrella Policies

- Standard primary policy liability limits ($300K to $500K) are far below typical net worth for high-income borrowers in this market.

- Liability concentration on properties with pools, docks, acreage, and recreational features (zip lines, dirt-bike trails, ATV use) requires elevated umbrella consideration.

### 10.5 Insurance Binder Timing in Jumbo Closings

Insurance-binder timing in jumbo closings runs earlier than in agency closings because investor scrutiny is higher.

- Investor requirements include verification of replacement-cost coverage equal to or exceeding the loan amount.
- Lender mortgagee-clause requirements are universal, but the wording requirements (loss-payee provisions, additional-insured provisions) are sometimes specific to the investor.
- Expect the insurance binder to be requested no later than 7 to 10 days before close, sometimes earlier.

### 11.1 The Alabama Advantage

Alabama has the lowest effective property tax rate in the country. ### 11.2 County-Level Millage Variation

The Madison County millage structure is anchored at $3.35 per $100 assessed value on land and $3.65 on improvements outside any municipality. Property taxes inside municipalities add city millage. Per the Madison County Tax Collector's published rates (https://www.madisoncountyal.gov/departments/tax-collector/frequently-asked-questions, accessed 05-09-2026):

- City of Huntsville: $5.80 per $100 assessed value

- Triana: $6.15 per $100 assessed value
- New Hope, Gurley, Owens Cross Roads: $4.05 per $100 assessed value
- Outside any municipality: $3.35 (land) and $3.65 (improvements)

Limestone, Morgan, Marshall, and Jackson county equivalents are published at each county Revenue Commissioner's office. The writer should pull each county's current schedule before any cross-county comparison.

A 7% annual cap on assessment increases (Act 2024-344, effective 2025) limits year-over-year growth in assessed values regardless of market value increases (sourced from propertytaxrecords.alabamaofficialrecords.com summary, accessed 05-09-2026).

### 11.3 Homestead Exemption at Higher Price Points

Alabama's homestead exemption mechanics (per Alabama Department of Revenue, https://www.revenue.alabama.gov/property-tax/homestead-exemptions/, accessed 05-09-2026):

- **Homestead Exemption 1 (H1)**: Available to all Alabama owner-occupants of single-family residences regardless of age. $4,000 in assessed value exempt for state taxes; $2,000 in assessed value exempt for county taxes. The dollar value of the H1 exemption at jumbo prices is modest (approximately $40 to $60 in annual savings depending on millage, per propertytaxrates.org Alabama summary, accessed 05-09-2026: https://propertytaxrates.org/exemptions/alabama/).
- **Homestead Exemption 2 (H2)**: Age 65+ with annual adjusted gross income on the most recent state return less than $12,000. Limited applicability to jumbo borrowers.
- **Homestead Exemption 3 (H3)**: Age 65+ with combined taxable income of $12,000 or less on the most recent federal return, OR retired permanently and totally disabled regardless of age. Full exemption from state, county, and school property taxes. Limited applicability to typical jumbo borrowers but relevant to certain retiring federal civilians and disabled-veteran borrowers.
- **Homestead Exemption 4 (H4)**: Age 65+ regardless of income with combined taxable income greater than $12,000 on most recent federal return AND adjusted gross income greater than $12,000 on most recent state return. This is for the state portion of ad valorem taxes plus $2,000 of assessed value on county taxes. Available to most age-65+ jumbo borrowers, but the dollar value at jumbo prices is modest.
- **H4 disabled veteran (100% service-connected disability)**: Full exemption from all state, county, and school property tax on homestead, no income limit. File with the county Revenue Commissioner with VA documentation. Materially valuable to qualifying disabled-veteran jumbo borrowers.

The county-by-county filing process requires application at the local Revenue Commissioner's office. Filing must be done by December 31 of each year for the upcoming tax year (effective on October 1 ownership status).

### 11.4 Property Tax as a Relocation Driver

The combined state-and-local tax picture for high earners in Alabama includes:

- No state tax on Social Security benefits
- No state tax on military retirement
- No state tax on certain federal civilian retirement (teachers, U.S. civil service, federal railroad workers)
- Capital gains taxed at the same graduated income tax rates (top marginal rate 5%)
- Effective property tax rate among the lowest in the country

For inbound retirees and senior executives transitioning into retirement, this combination is structurally favorable. **All tax planning questions route to a CPA.**

## 12. AHFA, FHA, USDA, and VA Interaction with Jumbo

### 12.1 AHFA Programs

The Alabama Housing Finance Authority operates two flagship homeownership programs (AHFA, "First Step vs. Step Up": https://www.ahfa.com/news/first-step-vs-step-up-which-program-is-the-best-path-for-your-buyer, accessed 05-09-2026):

- **AHFA Step Up**: $97,300 maximum qualifying income limit (statewide, no purchase-price cap explicitly, but the program is paired with conforming first-mortgage products), 4% of purchase price as down-payment-assistance second mortgage, minimum credit score 640, max DTI 45%, 30-year fixed first mortgage.
- **AHFA First Step (Mortgage Revenue Bond)**: Lower-than-market interest rates, location-based income limits up to $161,700 in select areas effective 06-02-2025 (NCSHA bulletin: https://www.ncsha.org/hfa-news/new-income-and-sales-price-limits-effective-june-2-open-more-doors-for-ahfa-homebuyer-assistance/, accessed 05-09-2026), maximum sales price $665,173 in target areas and $544,233 in non-target areas.
- **Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC)**: Federal tax credit equal to 20% of annual mortgage interest paid up to $2,000 per year, in effect for the life of the original mortgage. Can be combined with Step Up.

For jumbo borrowers in this market: both Step Up and First Step programs sit well below the $832,750 conforming threshold and are not structurally relevant to typical jumbo transactions. The First Step purchase-price limits are explicit caps. The Step Up income limit is a hard ceiling that disqualifies most jumbo-eligible income profiles.

A jumbo borrower might still consider AHFA programs only in the rare scenario where the borrower buys a second property below the AHFA caps (for example, a moderate-priced rental for income property purposes); this is uncommon and the universal mechanics file's investment-property framework is the dominant analysis.

**AHFA Salute Our Soldiers**: The AHFA Salute Our Soldiers program (https://www.ahfa.com/programs/homeownership) targets active-duty military, veterans, and surviving spouses with similar income and purchase-price constraints. Same conclusion: jumbo borrowers are typically not eligible because of price caps. The writer should verify current SOS program parameters before citing specifics.

### 12.2 FHA in High-Priced Submarkets

The FHA loan limit in most areas of Alabama in 2026 is the FHA floor of $541,287 (HUD, "FHA 2026 Loan Limits Announcement HUD No. 25-145": https://www.hud.gov/news/hud-no-25-145, accessed 05-09-2026; LendingTree Alabama FHA loan limits: https://www.lendingtree.com/home/fha/fha-loan-limits-in-alabama/, accessed 05-09-2026). FHA is generally not structurally relevant in high-priced submarkets above this loan amount.

### 12.3 USDA in High-Priced Submarkets

USDA Rural Development single-family direct loan limits in Alabama for 2026 sit at $324,700 in Madison, Marshall, and most surrounding counties (USDA RD: https://www.rd.usda.gov/files/RD-SFHAreaLoanLimitMap.pdf, accessed 05-09-2026). Income and purchase-price limits exclude jumbo borrowers from the USDA program.

### 12.4 VA at Jumbo Loan Amounts

This is the most leveraged universal-mechanics interaction with the local jumbo market.

The Blue Water Navy Vietnam Veterans Act of 2019, effective 01-01-2020, eliminated the VA loan-amount cap for veterans with full entitlement (VA, "Blue Water Navy Veterans and Your Home Loan Benefits": https://www.benefits.va.gov/homeloans/bwnact.asp, accessed 05-09-2026; VA Circular 6-19-30: https://aiqresourceguide.acesaudit.com/pages/viewpage.action?pageId=626786308). The VA guarantees 25% of any loan amount above $144,000 to lenders for full-entitlement borrowers. Lenders willing to underwrite the loan can do so with no required down payment regardless of price.

Practical math: a senior officer or senior NCO with full entitlement, no prior VA loan in use, qualifying credit, and qualifying income can purchase at $900,000 or $1,200,000 with no down payment, paying only the VA funding fee (waivable for service-connected disabled veterans, Purple Heart recipients on active duty per the Act, and qualifying surviving spouses).

When VA is structurally better than jumbo at higher loan amounts:

- Borrower has full entitlement, qualifying credit, and qualifying income.
- No-down-payment requirement is meaningful for the borrower's overall planning.
- No mortgage insurance on VA versus typical jumbo mortgage insurance avoidance through 20% to 25% down payment.
- VA funding fee is waivable due to service-connected disability, in which case the closing-cost differential is favorable.

When jumbo is structurally better than VA at higher loan amounts:

- Borrower has only partial entitlement and would need a sizable down payment to bridge the entitlement gap.
- Property is a second home, lake home, or other non-primary use case (VA is primary-residence only).
- Borrower's credit profile is at the upper end where universal jumbo investors price more aggressively than VA.
- Property is part of an investment-portfolio strategy.

The bonus or second-tier entitlement scenario applies when a veteran with an active VA loan elsewhere wants to use remaining entitlement on a new VA purchase in this market. The new loan's maximum size depends on remaining entitlement and the conforming loan limit in the property's county; in this market that is the $832,750 baseline. Cross-reference the universal VA mechanics file.

Property tax is one of the structural advantages of relocating into Alabama from a higher-tax state.

**Q: Can I get a jumbo VA loan in Huntsville with no down payment?**
A: Yes, if you have full VA entitlement. Since January 1, 2020, the Blue Water Navy Vietnam Veterans Act of 2019 eliminated the VA loan-amount cap for full-entitlement veterans. The lender must still underwrite to its credit and income standards. Veterans with partial entitlement may need a down payment to bridge the gap.

**Q: Can I use my California or Bay Area home equity to buy in Madison, Alabama?**
A: Yes, once the proceeds arrive in your account with documented seasoning. Until the sale closes and funds arrive, the equity is an expectation, not a documented asset. If your buy-side closing is before the sell-side proceeds arrive, you may need bridge financing or a HELOC on the departing residence to cover cash-to-close.

**Q: Should I use the relocation provider's recommended lender for my jumbo?**
A: Compare the provider's recommended lender against at least one local jumbo specialist. Provider-recommended lenders are usually national-brand lenders with relocation-program experience but not necessarily the strongest local appraiser-pool connections or jumbo overlays.

**Q: What submarkets in Huntsville and Madison most often involve jumbo loans?**
A: Hampton Cove (especially McMullen Cove, Stoneybrook, Heritage Plantation), Twickenham Historic District, Monte Sano, Green Mountain, the Ledges, Madison's Clift's Cove and upper-tier subdivisions, downtown Huntsville luxury infill, and lake homes on Smith Lake and Lake Guntersville. New construction in luxury subdivisions has pushed more transactions above the conforming line in the past 24 to 36 months.

**Q: Are AHFA programs available for jumbo borrowers in North Alabama?**
A: Generally no. The AHFA Step Up program has a $97,300 income limit, and the AHFA First Step Mortgage Revenue Bond program has a maximum sales price of $665,173 in target areas and $544,233 in non-target areas. Jumbo borrowers typically exceed at least one of these limits.

**Q: Is the FORTIFIED Roof grant available in Madison County?**
A: As of late 2025 and early 2026, the Strengthen Alabama Homes program had been historically available in Mobile and Baldwin counties, with 2025 expansion to Jefferson, Tuscaloosa, and Escambia counties. Madison, Limestone, Morgan, Marshall, Jackson, DeKalb, and Cullman counties were not yet on the SAH grant schedule. IBHS FORTIFIED designation itself is achievable through a self-funded retrofit and drives insurance discounts independent of grant eligibility. Verify the current quarterly grant schedule at strengthenalabamahomes.com.

**Q: Do I need two appraisals on a $1.5 million home in Hampton Cove?**
A: It depends on the investor and lender. Most jumbo investors trigger a two-appraisal requirement at $1.5 million or $2 million. The lender will use the lower of the two values. Two appraisals add cost (one extra appraisal fee) and a week to ten days of timeline. In comp-scarce submarkets like Hampton Cove, McMullen Cove, Twickenham, and lake luxury, two-appraisal triggers are routine.

**Q: How does the 2026 Madison City Schools rezoning affect home values in Madison?**
A: This packet does not assert school-district effects on home value. Buyers should verify the post-rezoning attendance zone for any specific address with Madison City Schools directly before assuming continuity. Students entering certain grades may request to remain at their current school but parents become responsible for transportation. The MCS School Locator is the authoritative source.

## 18. Risks, Caveats, and Overclaiming Language to Avoid

### 18.1 Fair Housing Pitfalls (highest priority)

Statements that should never appear in published content built from this packet:

- "Best neighborhood," "most desirable area," "the best schools," "the right kind of community," "family-friendly," "exclusive area"
- Any description of a submarket that implies protected-class composition
- Any human-model imagery that would suggest a preference for one protected class over another (HUD FHEO regulations on human models in housing advertising remain in effect, sourced from the Fair Housing Advertising Manual long-standing guidance: http://www.mvfairhousing.com/realtors/files/Fair%20Housing%20Advertising%20Manual.PDF, accessed 05-09-2026)
- Steering language that directs buyers based on assumed protected-class preferences

Statements that can appear with care, factually and with sourcing:

- "Madison City Schools attendance zone (verify with the district)"
- "Homes in this subdivision feed into [school name] per the current attendance zone map at [district URL]"
- Factual market data with sourcing
- Factual amenity references (size, year built, programs offered)

The HUD Office of Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity 04-29-2024 advertising guidance is the most recent material federal guidance in this area and applies to digital advertising, AI-driven targeting, and ad-platform delivery (HUD FHEO, 04-29-2024: https://www.fhcci.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/FHEO_Guidance_on_Advertising_through_Digital_Platforms-4-29-24.pdf, accessed 05-09-2026).

### 18.2 Luxury Marketing Pitfalls

Avoid:

- "VIP service" without verifiable service distinction
- "Concierge service" without a defined concierge program
- "White-glove experience" without verifiable distinct treatment
- "Exclusive jumbo programs" without an actually exclusive (lender-specific, not universally available) program
- "We work with all the luxury builders" without verification
- "Guaranteed close by your PCS / start date" (never as a guarantee)
- "Our lender has the lowest rates" without a verifiable basis

### 18.3 Relocation Marketing Pitfalls

Avoid:

- Tax claims that imply the loan officer is providing tax advice (route to CPA)
- Bridge-loan guarantees on unspecified terms
- Concurrent-close timeline guarantees
- Statements that the relocation provider's preferred lender is the best fit for the buyer

### 18.4 Universal Disclaimers

- Equal Housing Lender disclosure
- NMLS disclosure (loan officer NMLS number and Princeton Mortgage NMLS company number)
- Alabama state mortgage license disclosure (Alabama Banking Department or Alabama Securities Commission as applicable)
- "May," "can," "depending on submarket," "subject to investor overlay" hedging where appropriate
- VA-specific advertising guidance for lenders applies whenever VA mechanics are referenced (route to VA Circular 26-19-30 / Circular 6-19-30 framework and to current VA advertising guidance at https://benefits.va.gov/HOMELOANS/lenders_circulars.asp)
- CFPB-flagged misleading-advertising patterns: see https://www.consumerfinance.gov/compliance/compliance-resources/mortgage-resources/

### 18.5 Routing for non-mortgage questions

- All tax questions: "consult a CPA"
- All financial-planning questions: "consult a financial advisor"
- All entity-structure and trust questions: "consult an attorney"
- All concurrent-closing legal coordination questions: "the closing attorneys in both states"
- All corporate relocation package questions: "the relocation provider, employer, and a CPA experienced with multi-state relocation"
- All school decisions: "verify attendance zone with the school district directly"
- All appraisal-outcome speculation: "appraisal outcomes cannot be guaranteed and are subject to investor and lender review"
